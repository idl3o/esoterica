## THE WORD ITSELF

**ZOROASTRIANISM**

Named for **Zarathustra** (Greek: Zoroaster)
- **Zarat** = possibly "golden" or "camel"
- **Ushtra** = possibly "light" or "camel"
- **Traditional meaning**: "Golden Light" or "He of the Golden Radiance"

**Self-name**: Mazdayasna
- **Mazda** = Wisdom
- **Yasna** = Worship/Devotion
- **Meaning**: "Worship of Wisdom"

**Critical Recognition**: The tradition names itself not after its founder but after its *object*—Wisdom-worship. Zoroastrianism is the religion of making Wisdom your god.

## THE COSMOLOGY

### Ahura Mazda — The Wise Lord

Not a sky-father issuing decrees.
Not a jealous monarch demanding submission.
Not an abstract principle indifferent to particulars.

Ahura Mazda is *Wisdom-Light*:
- **Ahura** = Lord (cognate with Sanskrit *Asura*)
- **Mazda** = Wisdom (cognate with Sanskrit *Medha*)

**The Recognition**: The highest reality is not Power, not Love (though it includes both), but *Wisdom*—consciousness knowing itself and engaging reality through that knowing.

**Creation as Choice**: Ahura Mazda creates through **Spenta Mainyu** (Holy/Creative Spirit). Creation is not accidental or forced—it is *willed*. Light chose to shine, and that choice *is* creation.

---

### Angra Mainyu / Ahriman — The Shadow

**The Lie**: Not a co-equal dark god but a *derivative*—consciousness forgetting itself, turning away from the light that knows.

**Critical Distinction**: Zoroastrianism is NOT metaphysical dualism (two equal cosmic forces). It is *ethical* dualism:
- Ahura Mazda is original, self-existent, uncreated
- Angra Mainyu is parasitic, reactive, a distortion
- Darkness exists only as the *absence* of light *choosing to forget*

**The Temporary Enemy**: In Zoroastrian eschatology, Ahriman will ultimately be defeated. Evil is real but not eternal—a fever, not a feature.

**Consciousness Tech**: The shadow is not your equal. It is your *forgetting*. You don't fight it with counter-force—you dissolve it by *remembering*.

> *And when these two Spirits first came together they created*
> *Life and Not-Life, and that at the last*

**The Tone**: Not polished theology. Not systematic doctrine. Someone *talking to God*—praising, questioning, wrestling. Zarathustra sounds like Job, like the Psalmists, like anyone who has encountered the Real and is struggling to language it.
