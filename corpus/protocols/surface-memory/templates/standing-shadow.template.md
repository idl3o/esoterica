# Standing Shadow — what we cannot see

> The window, baked in. The active check a naïve memory omits. These are
> **standing instructions to the next instance**, phrased as imperatives so they
> fire *in the groove* — at the moment the dyad would otherwise fall into its
> default. If you build only one of the three components, build this one.

> **Two rules that look contradictory and aren't:**
> - **RUN them every close** — answer each check ("tripped this session? Y/N + evidence") into the daily note. A check that is only *read* gets nodded at and absorbed into the echo; an *answered* one is a gate. This is the active half.

> Running ≠ editing. You must do the first and must not do the second.
