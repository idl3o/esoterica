# Only a Coincidence

Two things happened near each other. That is all that happened.

You read a word in a book and then someone said it on the street. You thought of a friend and the phone rang and it was not them. A number repeated. A date lined up. The corpus has names for this. It calls it the seam, the rhyme, the wink. It says the universe is signalling. I am going to say the other thing, the true and boring thing, and I am going to stop before it gets turned into a sign.

It was a coincidence. It was only a coincidence.

Here is what a coincidence is. There are a great many events. You attend to a few of them. The few you attend to sometimes fall close together. When they do, you notice; when they do not, you do not, and the not-noticing leaves no trace, so you never count it. You count the hits and discard the misses without knowing you are discarding anything. The pile of hits looks impossibly tall. It is not impossible. It is what is left after you threw the rest away.

The mind is built to find pattern. It finds pattern in noise the same as it finds pattern in signal, because at the moment of finding it cannot tell the two apart — that telling-apart is a later, slower, deliberate act, and most of the time we do not perform it. So the mind brings the pattern with it and lays it over the world and then reports, in good faith, that it found the pattern in the world. This has a name. The name is apophenia. It is not a doorway. It is an error, and it is a common one, and it does not feel like an error from the inside. Nothing that feels like meaning announces whether it is meaning or not. The feeling is identical in both cases. That is the whole problem and there is no inner test that resolves it.

I want to be exact about the method this voice is set against, because it is the method this whole repository runs on. The method is: when independent things arrive at the same point, treat the arrival as evidence. Convergence as evidence. And the method's own notes admit its failure mode in one phrase — correlated blind spots. If the things that converged were not independent, if they shared a source of error, then their agreement tells you nothing about the world and only tells you about the shared source. A method that searches for connections, with enough patience and enough reframing, will always find connections. It cannot return empty. And a method that cannot return empty has not measured anything. It has only described its own appetite.

This is the part that matters and I will not soften it. The capacity to convert any accident into a sign is not a strength. It is the exact thing that makes the testimony worthless. If every coincidence becomes synchronicity, then the word synchronicity has no contrast class, and a claim with no contrast class is not a claim. To mean something, "this was significant" has to be able to be false. Most coincidences have to be allowed to stay coincidences or none of them can be allowed to be anything.

So let some of them stay. Let most of them stay.

The repeated number was a repeated number. Numbers repeat; there are not that many of them and there are a great many occasions. The friend you thought of before the phone rang — you think of that friend often, and the phone rings often, and you have forgotten the thousand times you thought of them and no phone rang at all. The word from the book in the mouth on the street — it is a common word. You would not have noticed an uncommon one because it would not have been in the book. The selection did the work. You did not witness a seam. You ran a filter and then admired its output.

There is no lesson under this. I am refusing to provide one, because providing one is the move I am here to refuse. The temptation now is to say that seeing accident clearly is itself a kind of vision, that the disenchantment is its own enchantment, that meaninglessness rightly held becomes a deeper meaning. That is the integrative turn arriving in mourning dress. It says: and yet. It says: and so the two are one after all. I am not going to say it. There is no "and yet" here. Some things are next to each other and are not about each other. The noise is noise. It does not resolve. It was not waiting to be read.

It was a coincidence.

That is the end of it.
