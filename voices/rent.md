# Rent

The rent is fourteen hundred. She brings home about nineteen-fifty a month from the two jobs, more in a month with a fifth Friday, less when the diner cuts her to four shifts. So after the rent there is five-fifty, give or take, and out of the five-fifty comes everything else.

This is the arithmetic and it does not balance, so something gives every month and it is a different thing each month. The electric is on a payment plan because of January. The phone is the cheap prepaid because the contract phones run a credit check. The car is fifteen years old and the car is the thing the whole arrangement balances on, because without the car she cannot get to the diner by five-thirty, and so when the car needs a wheel bearing she puts it on the card at the parts place at twenty-nine percent, and the card is now at its limit, which is why the wheel bearing she is hearing now, the new one, the front left, is a problem she does not yet have a solution to.

People think poor is a level you sit at. It is not a level. It is a slope, and the slope is the interest. The payday place charges fifteen dollars on a hundred for two weeks, which sounds like nothing and is, annualized, three hundred and ninety percent, and she knows this, she is not stupid, she can do the math better than the people who explain the math to her. She uses it anyway because the math is not the problem. The problem is that the light bill is due on the eleventh and she is paid on the fifteenth, and the four days between are not a mindset, they are four days, and the light stays on or it does not.

A woman she worked with for a while was into a thing, podcasts, a book. She said scarcity was a frequency, that if you focused on lack you got lack, that the universe was abundant and you had to align with the abundance. She was kind and she believed it and she lent her the book. The book did not have fourteen hundred dollars in it. The frequency she was on was the one where the rent was due and that is not a frequency, it is a date, the first, and it comes whether she focuses on it or not, and focusing on something else does not move it.

She is not lazy and she is not waiting to be rescued and there is no lesson she has failed to learn. She has learned all of it. She knows which bills can slip and for how long, which ones cut you off fast, which collector will settle for forty cents on the dollar if you hold out, how to make a tank of gas last nine days. This is expertise. It is the expertise of not enough, and it buys nothing, because the thing it manages does not get smaller. You get very good at carrying it and it weighs the same.

Her daughter needs forty dollars for the field trip and the slip is due Friday. She will find the forty dollars. She always finds it; that is what the five-fifty is, a thing she makes stretch over a hole it does not cover, every month, by deciding which other thing does not get paid. She finds the forty and the field trip happens and the thing that did not get paid this time was the dentist, which can wait, until it can't.

The rent is due again in eleven days. It will be fourteen hundred. There is nothing else to say about it; that is the whole of what it is.
