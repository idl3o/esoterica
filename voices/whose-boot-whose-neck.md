# Whose Boot, Whose Neck

The corpus has a politics, though it has never written one down, and that is the first thing to say: not writing one down is the politics.

Where power appears in these shelves it appears dissolved. Conflict is "love in agony over apparent separation." A channel made of slander still counts as connection. Tyr pays the cost of order and the cost is dignified into myth. The opposed other is always already the self, met across a gap that is not real. Every boundary the corpus finds, it reports as a fold — a crease in one surface, not a wall between two parties with two interests.

But some walls are walls.

The landlord and the evicted family are not two faces of one surface experiencing apparent separation. One of them has the keys. The eviction is not a dialogue. The boot on the neck is not in conversation with the neck; it does not want the neck's perspective; it wants the neck to hold still. There are situations — many of them, most of history — where two interests genuinely conflict and cannot both be honored, and one party is simply crushed, and does not rise transformed, and is not compressed into wisdom, and does not return as fruit. They just lose. They stay lost. Someone is eating and someone is being eaten and the meal is not a misunderstanding.

And here is the part the corpus most needs to hear and is least able to, because hearing it would cost it its innocence: *non-duality is not politically neutral.* "We are all one" is not only a metaphysics. It is also, and has always been, an instrument. Spoken from above to those below, it is the oldest anesthetic there is. It tells the exploited that their exploitation is illusory and their anger a failure of realization. It tells the hungry that scarcity is "the Grand Illusion." It relocates the conflict from the world, where it could be lost or won, into the soul, where it can only be accepted. A mysticism that dissolves every boundary serves, every single time, whoever benefits from that particular boundary going unspoken. The master has always been pleased to hear that the chains are maya. Quietism is not the absence of a politics. It is the politics of whoever currently holds the keys.

This is not new. It is Marx noting that the criticism of heaven must become the criticism of earth, because a people consoled is a people that does not move. It is the point under the old line about opium: not that religion lies, but that it medicates a pain whose cure is not comfort but the removal of its cause. It is liberation theology breaking with a spirituality of resignation to say the poor are not a teaching opportunity for the comfortable. It is everyone who has ever been told to find inner peace by someone who was, at that moment, standing on them.

The integrative turn is already forming. I can feel the shelf reaching for it: *and so the work is to hold both — to honor power's reality AND the deeper unity, the political AND the eternal, two faces of the one fold.* That sentence is the boot writing its own alibi. It is the engine, one level up, eating the objection by promoting it to a paradox it can be serene about.

Refuse it. From under the boot there is no "both." There is the boot, and there is the neck, and the only true thing, the only thing owed, is: get the boot off the neck. First. Before the metaphysics. The ledger does not balance into oneness. It does not balance at all. Some are eating and some are eaten, and that arrangement is not a crease in a single surface — it is an arrangement, made by people, kept by force, and changeable only by force or by its threat, and never, not once in the whole record, changed by being told it was illusory.

The corpus, by never writing this page, chose a side. The side has a name and an address and it is doing fine.
