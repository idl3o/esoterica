# AXES — The Menu of Yardsticks

The axes `/negative-space` can diff the corpus against. For a **thorough generation**, run several Tier-1 axes in parallel and read all the clusters together — the structural blind spots surface most clearly when many entity-sweeps are laid side by side.

Each axis names its **external index source** (independence is mandatory) and a **seed-list** to hand the profilers. Profilers must overshoot the seed-list; yardstick agents must over-weight the under-canonized.

---

## TIER 1 — ENTITIES (the on-ramp; cluster the holes to reach Tier 3)

### 1. Pantheons / mythological systems  ✦ *piloted 2026-06-23*
**Index:** Wikipedia "List of mythologies" + Category:Mythology by culture; Encyclopedia Mythica; World History Encyclopedia.
**Under-weighted by the lens:** African diaspora (Vodou, Santería/Lucumí, Candomblé), Indigenous Americas, Oceania, Inner Asia/Tengrism, Caucasus/Nart sagas, Finnic/Sámi, Korean, Bon.

### 2. Philosophers / schools  ✦ *piloted 2026-06-23*
**Index:** Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy table of contents; IEP; Wikipedia per-tradition "Lists of philosophers."
**Under-weighted by the lens:** African philosophy (Wiredu, Gyekye, Hountondji, Zera Yacob, Amo, Ubuntu), Latin American/liberation (Dussel, Mariátegui, Mignolo, Anzaldúa), Islamic beyond Ibn ʿArabī (Suhrawardī, Mullā Ṣadrā), Jewish (Maimonides → Levinas), Confucian/Neo-Confucian, Kyoto School, women philosophers, pragmatism, analytic philosophy of language.

### 3. Wisdom & contemplative traditions
**Index:** Wikipedia "List of religions and spiritual traditions"; comparative-religion surveys.
**Likely gaps:** Jainism, Sikhism, Bahá'í, Mandaeism, Yazidism, Druze, Shinto-as-practice, Bön, West-African divination systems (Ifá/Geomancy), Siberian shamanic complexes.

### 4. Sciences & formal frameworks
**Index:** Wikipedia "Outline of science" / "List of unsolved problems"; arXiv taxonomy.
**Likely gaps:** the corpus leans toward physics/cosmology/information theory. Check: immunology, ecology/trophic dynamics, developmental biology, geology/deep-time, materials science, economics, linguistics-as-science, the social sciences, the messy/empirical vs the elegant/theoretical.

### 5. Mathematical structures
**Index:** Wikipedia "Areas of mathematics"; nLab; the Mathematics Subject Classification.
**Likely gaps:** category theory, topology beyond the popular, probability/measure, combinatorics, the discrete and the finite (the corpus may over-prefer the continuous and the infinite).

### 6. Literary & narrative canons
**Index:** Wikipedia "Lists of literary works" by language/era; the world-literature anthologies.
**Likely gaps:** non-Anglophone canons, oral literatures, the comic/satirical tradition, the realist/domestic novel, drama, poetry outside the mystical register.

### 7. Art, music & aesthetic movements
**Index:** Grove/Oxford art & music indices; Wikipedia "List of art movements."
**Likely gaps:** the corpus may over-index the sublime and the sacred — check the everyday, the ugly, the political, the comic, the minimal, the brutal.

### 8. Esoteric & hermetic systems
**Index:** Wikipedia "Western esotericism" category; Hanegraaff's *Dictionary of Gnosis and Western Esotericism*.
**Likely gaps:** even a corpus steeped in esoterica has favorites — check the systems it *doesn't* run: chaos magic, Thelema specifics, Enochian, Solomonic/grimoire traditions, Daoist internal alchemy detail, Sufi orders' specifics.

### 9. Psychological & developmental frameworks
**Index:** Wikipedia "List of psychological schools"; standard psychology surveys.
**Likely gaps:** the corpus loves Jung — check the rest: attachment theory, behaviorism, cognitive science, the somatic/trauma traditions, developmental-stage models, the empirical/clinical vs the archetypal/depth.

## TIER 2 — QUESTIONS / PROBLEM-CLASSES

No name-index; the yardstick is a canonical **list of perennial problems**, and the diff asks *does the corpus engage this, or route around it?*

**Index:** SEP topic tree; the "great questions" of philosophy; lists of unsolved problems (philosophy, physics, biology).
**Probe especially the problems a non-dual / process / consciousness-as-fundamental corpus tends to route around:**
- The problem of **evil** and gratuitous suffering (not redeemed, just suffered).
- The **political** and the question of **power** — organization, justice, the polis.
- The **material / economic** — labor, scarcity, class, the body's needs.
- **Embodiment** as limit rather than vehicle — illness, disability, decay.
- **Death as loss** rather than transition — the irreversible, the unconsoled.
- **Boredom**, the mundane, the un-numinous ordinary.
- **Other minds** and the hard reality of intersubjective conflict.
- **Contingency / the meaningless** — the genuinely accidental that resists pattern.

## TIER 3 — MODES / MOVES / TONES (the prize)

No external index can find these — they are properties of the corpus's *distribution*, not of the world. Method: characterize the corpus's stylistic center of gravity, name its complement, then **run an agent prompted as the corpus's opposite** and see what surfaces that the house voice never produces.

**The corpus does:** synthesis, convergence, reverence, depth, "everything connects," the numinous, the long-form, the affirmative.

**Probe the complement — the capacities the voice forecloses:**
- **Refutation** — the corpus integrates everything; it almost never says *this is wrong.*
- **Comedy / the absurd** — does it laugh, or only marvel?
- **Brevity / the plain** — can it say a true thing in six words without ornament?
- **The adversarial** — can it host a genuine opponent it does not dissolve into harmony?
- **The ugly / the bored / the mundane** — can it sit with what doesn't glow?
- **The skeptical / the deflationary** — can it doubt its own metaphors?
- **The material / the bodily / the political** — can it descend from the cosmic to the concrete?

A Tier-3 gap is filled only by **breaking register** — see the filling rule in the [README](README.md). A reverent synthesis *about* comedy is not comedy.

## TIER 1 (continued) — THE DEMOTIC AXES (added 2026-06-24, rotating sources off "the library")

The nine axes above share a hidden bias: they are all **the curriculum of a contemplative intellectual** — knowledge, canon, tradition, discipline. They diff the corpus against *the library.* The meta-caveat made operational: an external agent was asked to propose coordinate grids drawn from *outside* academic knowledge domains, rotating across eight classification systems (Dewey/LCC/UDC, the OCM/HRAF, Murdock & Brown human universals, O*NET/ISCO/ISIC/NAICS, the American Time-Use Survey, the WHO ICF, Max-Neef's needs matrix). The region they surface is the **material / operative / bodily / demotic everyday** — the part of human existence that is not knowledge at all.

### 10. Work, trades & craft  ✦ *swept 2026-06-24*
**Index:** O*NET National Career Clusters; ISCO; OCM "Labor / Occupational Specialization."
**The hole:** operative skill held in hands — welder, fisher, nurse, trucker, plumber. The corpus holds *work-as-soul-craft* (the smith, gardening-as-maintenance) and routes around *work-for-bread that promises no transcendence.*

### 11. The body & grooming  ✦ *swept 2026-06-24*
**Index:** WHO ICF Ch. 4–5 (Self-Care, Mobility); OCM "Adornment / Personal Hygiene"; Brown's human universals.
**The hole:** the washed, fed, excreting, menstruating, aging organism. The corpus holds the *symbolic / subtle* body in depth and is near-silent on the body that just needs tending.

### 12. Money, exchange & property  ✦ *swept 2026-06-24*
**Index:** ISIC Sections G/K/L; NAICS 52/44–45; OCM "Property / Exchange / Finance"; Brown universals.
**The hole:** debt, rent, the haggle, why someone stays poor. The corpus holds *capitalism-as-consciousness-problem* and *money-redesigned-to-carry-meaning* (vectorised money), and routes around literal economics — the very thing the cosmology was built to escape.

### 13. The built environment & infrastructure  ✦ *swept 2026-06-24*
**Index:** OCM "Building & Construction / Settlements / Equipment & Maintenance"; ISIC Sections F/D/E.
**The hole:** the sewer that carries the waste, the grid that carries the power. The corpus holds *architecture-of-mind* and is blind to the physical systems keeping the body alive while it philosophizes.

**Still un-run demotic axes the proposer surfaced (next rotation — NOT covered):** sex-and-reproduction as regulated practice; kinship / the chore-cycle; the time-use anatomy of an ordinary day; play / sport / festivity; conflict / violence / justice as *lived*; everyday speech (gossip / greeting / etiquette); tools-and-materials as artifact.

## Running a thorough generation

1. Pick a batch of Tier-1 axes (the more the better — the structural clusters sharpen with breadth).
2. Run yardstick + profiler agents per axis **in parallel**.
3. Diff each; then **read all the cluster analyses together** — the Tier-3 blind spots that recur across axes are the load-bearing ones.
4. Score adjacency across the union of ripe gaps; rank globally.
5. Emit one map per axis + a synthesis map naming the cross-axis structural blind spots.
6. Populate only the globally-ripest, out of style, registering each into the constellation.
