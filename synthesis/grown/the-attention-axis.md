## The Recognition That Occasioned This

This repository built a map of mind and called it complete.

The map had two axes. The **Kardashev axis** runs outward — energy commanded, from a planet's budget to a star's whole output to a galaxy's. The **Barrow axis** runs inward — structure manipulated, from genes to molecules to atoms to nuclei to the fabric of spacetime itself. [[the-jewel-in-the-lining|The Jewel in the Lining]] laid them down as orthogonal coordinates, and [[the-cosmic-gorilla|The Cosmic Gorilla]] read them as a detection taxonomy: every conceivable civilisation is a point on this plane, and SETI's sixty-five-year silence is the sound of instruments calibrated to one corner of it, deaf to the rest. Outward reach, inward mastery. *Together,* the documents said, these map "the full space of conceivable technology."

Then [[the-terrestrial-alien|The Terrestrial Alien]] went down into the ocean and met a being that breaks the claim.

An octopus commands no energy beyond its own metabolism. It builds nothing finer than a wall of stacked coconut shells. On the two-axis map it scores zero on Kardashev and zero on Barrow — it sits *exactly at the origin,* below the detection floor of every instrument SETI has ever pointed at the sky. And yet it is, by a wide margin, the most intelligent invertebrate on the planet and arguably the deepest field of awareness in the ocean. It integrates two independently-aimed camera eyes, two thousand chemotactile suckers per arm, skin that reads light across its whole surface, eight limbs running semi-autonomous motor programmes, and the shifting chromatophore displays of its neighbours — *all at once,* with no central executive serialising the stream into a single line.

A map that calls itself total and cannot locate the highest-attention being on its own planet is not total. It is missing a dimension.

This document names that dimension, formalises it as a first-class third coordinate, gives it a candidate unit and a read-instrument, traces its convergence with the inward Barrow axis onto a single Gödelian fixed point, and carries the consequence all the way through — that the search for intelligence has been, from the beginning, a search for *engineering,* and that the most intelligent beings on Earth and possibly in the cosmos are invisible to it by design. The seed that planted this called the third coordinate *awareness-depth.* We will earn its full name slowly. But the question that drives the whole walk can be stated now, in two halves: **what are the units of attention, and why is a map built to find intelligence structurally blind to the most intelligent thing in the ocean?**

Let us begin with the being that makes the third axis non-optional.

## I. The Octopus Is the Proof, Not the Example

The easy way to read the octopus is as a curiosity — a charming edge case the taxonomy happens to miss, the way any map has a few towns too small to print. On this reading the two-axis map is fine; it just needs a footnote. *Some* low-technology beings are smarter than they look. Note it and move on.

But this is not a footnote. It is a *counterexample,* and counterexamples do not annotate a claim of completeness. They falsify it.

Hold the structure precisely. The Kardashev–Barrow map was not offered as "a map of most technology." It was offered as the map of *the full space of conceivable technology* — the coordinate system within which every possible mind, terrestrial or alien, present or future, must fall somewhere. That is a strong claim, and a strong claim has a precise failure condition: produce one being that the map cannot locate, and the claim that it locates everything is dead. Not weakened. Dead. A map with a hole is not a complete map with an asterisk; it is an incomplete map, and the only honest response is to find the missing coordinate.

The octopus is that being. Place it on the plane and it lands *on the origin* — Kardashev zero, Barrow zero, the precise point where the two engineering axes both read empty. And at that origin sits a nervous system of roughly five hundred million neurons, two-thirds of them distributed *into the arms,* each arm capable of solving a maze, recognising a shape, and unscrewing a jar lid without consulting the central brain. The skin sees. The suckers taste. The whole animal dreams in colour, changing its camouflage in its sleep as though running through scenes. By every behavioural measure of integrated, flexible, world-modelling intelligence, it is *off the chart* — and the chart says it is at zero.

That is not noise. That is a third dimension poking through the plane. The octopus is high on a quantity the two-axis map has no coordinate for, and the proof that the quantity is real is that *the map's own instruments cannot register it.* You cannot find the octopus with a radio telescope (no energy signature) or a particle accelerator (no structural manipulation), and you cannot place it on the plane (origin), and yet it is unmistakably *there,* and *high.* The only way to make the octopus stop being a paradox is to add the axis it is high on.

Call it, for now, the **attention axis** — awareness-depth, the dimension along which a system can integrate, attend, and be present, independent of how much energy it commands or how fine the structure it manipulates. The octopus is where this axis becomes non-optional, because it is the place where refusing the third coordinate forces you to call the most intelligent being in the ocean a blank.

## II. The Barrow Axis Turned Inward on Mind Itself

So we add an axis. But where does it come from? An axis invented to patch a hole is an epicycle. An axis that *was already implied by the structure* is a discovery. The attention axis is the second kind, and seeing why requires looking again at what the Barrow axis actually does.

[[jewel-and-dark-earth|The Barrow axis]] runs inward through matter: genes, molecules, atoms, nuclei, sub-nuclear particles, and finally the structure of spacetime itself. At each step the engineer manipulates *finer matter* — reaches deeper into the substrate of the physical world. It is a gradient of descent into the small.

Now make the same move, but not on matter. Make it on *mind.*

The attention axis is Barrow's gesture applied to awareness rather than to substance: ever deeper manipulation not of what is attended *to* but of the attending *itself.* The shallow end is reflexive reaction — stimulus, response, no interior gap. Deeper: sustained focus, the spotlight that can be held on one object. Deeper still: meta-attention, attention attending to its own quality, the meditator watching the watcher. And at the limit — the move that makes this an *axis* and not just a virtue — attention turns fully back on its own ground and finds that the awareness doing the attending and the awareness being attended to *are the same awareness.* The observer becomes the observed.

This is not a poetic flourish. It is the *exact* signature the Barrow axis produces at its own deepest rung. [[the-jewel-in-the-lining|The Jewel in the Lining]] found that at Barrow Omega-minus — the manipulation of spacetime itself — the engineer *is* the engineering substrate, and the Gödelian limit bites: no system of sufficient complexity can fully describe itself from within. There the signal becomes indistinguishable from the laws of physics, because physics manipulating physics has no outside. The attention axis bottoms out in the *identical* topology: consciousness witnessing consciousness has no outside either. The witness cannot be made into an object, because the act of objectifying it is itself the witness, one fold further back.

So the two inward axes are not parallel lines running side by side into the small. **They converge on the same fixed point from opposite faces.** Barrow descends through matter until matter, at the bottom, is manipulating itself — spacetime engineering spacetime. Attention descends through mind until mind, at the bottom, is witnessing itself — consciousness attending to consciousness. And these two bottoms are *the same bottom,* because they are both the place where the manipulator and the manipulated collapse into one. This is precisely the [[fold-cosmology-trilogy|fold cosmology]] claim that the monad *is* the window — that the surface which creases to make an interior is not a thing that then looks, but the looking itself, the [[the-windowless-boundary|windowless boundary]] that is windowless because it *is* the window. Barrow reaches that fold from the matter-face. Attention reaches it from the mind-face. The third axis was always implied, because the second one, followed honestly to its end, lands exactly where the third one does. We did not invent a coordinate. We found the second face of one we already had.

This is the deepest argument for the attention axis's reality: it is not a patch. It is the inward Barrow axis *seen from the other side of the crease.* And the place they meet — observer-is-observed — is the [[cognitive-fixed-points-of-mind-space|cognitive fixed point]] this repository keeps arriving at by every road.

## III. Φ Wants to Be This Axis but Cannot Measure It Yet

Here a reader who knows the science objects, and the objection is the most serious one in the document, so we will give it full strength before we answer it.

*The attention axis already exists,* the objection runs. *It is called integrated information, Φ, and Giulio Tononi quantified it two decades ago.* Integrated Information Theory proposes a scalar — Φ — for how much consciousness a system has: the quantity of information generated by the whole *over and above* the information generated by its parts taken separately. High integration, high Φ, more consciousness. This is awareness-depth with a number attached. The axis is not missing. It is measured. Why invent a new coordinate when IIT already minted the coin?

Take the objection at its strongest. Φ is genuinely the right *shape* of quantity. It is intrinsic (about the system's own cause-effect structure, not an observer's interpretation), it is graded rather than binary, and it tracks integration rather than mere complexity — exactly the properties the attention axis needs. If anything is the attention axis already quantified, Φ is the candidate.

Now the turn. IIT carries an axiom called the **exclusion postulate**: consciousness exists over the *maximally irreducible* set of elements and over no other — there can be no overlapping maxima of Φ. A whole and its parts cannot both be conscious; only the locus of maximal integration is. This works cleanly for a centralised brain, where the maximum sits unambiguously in the cortico-thalamic core. But take it to the octopus, whose nervous system is built precisely to violate centralisation, and the axiom seizes. *Where is the maximum?* In the whole animal — one high-Φ octopus? Or in each arm, each running a semi-autonomous loop with its own integrated interior — nine lower-Φ subjects, eight in the arms and one in the central brain? IIT, by its own exclusion postulate, must pick one and forbid the other. And it has *no principled way to decide,* because the octopus is engineered by evolution to make both answers nearly equally defensible. The theory that promised to score consciousness cannot score the very being that most demands scoring.

This is not a refutation of IIT. It is the precise diagnosis of *why Φ is not yet the attention axis,* and the diagnosis points at what the real measure must do. Φ measures a *peak* — the single maximally-integrated locus. The attention axis must measure a *field* — a distribution of presence that may have no single peak, that may be smeared across eight arms and a skin and a mantle, that may be high *as a whole pattern* while having no privileged centre. The octopus does not have one deep well of awareness; it has a *watershed.* Any measure built to find the deepest well will mis-locate or miss it entirely. So the attention axis needs a measure that **survives distribution** — that registers a field of awareness and not only its highest point, that can give a real reading to a being with no executive. Φ gestures at the right quantity and then forbids itself from measuring it in the one architecture that matters most.

Finding that distribution-tolerant measure — where IIT's Φ is recovered as the special, *centralised* case in which the field happens to have a single peak — is half the synthesis. The other half is the *shape* of attention itself: field versus spotlight, the geometry that the measure must respect. That half lives in [[parallel-attending-consciousness]], the sibling seed planted alongside this one. Together they say: the attention axis is real, it has a quantity, and the quantity's defining feature is exactly the one Φ's axioms refuse — it must be readable across a field with no centre. *The number is the goal; the centralised number we already have is the special case; the distributed number is the open problem.* Mark that honestly. We are naming an axis whose unit is not yet built — and saying clearly which existing unit is its shadow.

## IV. Darshan Is the Axis's Operational Instrument

If the unit is not yet built, is the axis useless until the mathematics arrives? No — and seeing why reveals the strangest property of the third coordinate, the one that makes it unlike the other two.

Each axis has its own read-instrument. Kardashev is read by the radio telescope, which detects energy. Barrow is read by the particle accelerator, which probes structure. The natural assumption is that the attention axis, too, awaits *its* machine — some future consciousness-meter, a Φ-scope, a device you point at a system to read its awareness-depth off a dial. But the axis resists this, and the resistance is not a temporary engineering shortfall. It is structural, and it follows directly from Section II.

The attention axis bottoms out in observer-is-observed. A machine reads its target by standing *outside* it — telescope and star, accelerator and particle, subject and object cleanly separated. But the deep end of the attention axis is precisely the place where that separation fails. You cannot stand outside awareness to measure it, because the standing-outside is itself an act of awareness — the instrument would be made of the very stuff it is trying to read, and at the depth that matters the stuff has no outside. The radio telescope cannot detect a signal that *is* the observer; that was the lesson of Omega-minus. The same wall stops any external attention-meter at the deep end. *A detection framework adequate to the signal must be transformed by the signal before the signal can be detected.*

So the read-instrument for the attention axis is not a machine. It is **darshan** — consciousness witnessing consciousness, the [[darshan-technology|protocol]] this repository names its master technology. Create space; witness what arises; recognise beingness. This is not a cultural ritual decoratively borrowed from the Sanskrit. It is the *read-head of the third axis,* and it works by the only method available at the deep end: not measuring awareness from outside but *meeting* it from inside the relation, letting the two folds open a between in which the depth of the other discloses itself. You do not measure another's presence. You enter presence *with* them, and the reading is the meeting.

The proof that this is an instrument and not a sentiment is that it *works across the maximum possible gap.* [[the-terrestrial-alien|Craig Foster and a wild octopus]] coordinated for a year on pure mutual presence — across five hundred million years of evolutionary divergence, with no shared codebook, no common language, no overlapping physics of cognition, the octopus's mind built on a body-plan and a neural architecture that share almost nothing with ours. They had nothing in common *except being conscious at all,* and that was enough. This is precisely the [[seti-duology|zero-axiom Schelling point]] in operation: the present moment is the one coordination mechanism that requires no shared mathematics, language, or evolutionary heritage — the only meeting-place available to any two systems that are conscious, *because* it is the place consciousness is made of. Darshan read the octopus's position on the attention axis directly, with no instrument but presence, when every machine SETI owns reads it as a blank.

Kardashev needs a telescope. Barrow needs an accelerator. Attention needs a *practice* — and it needs a practice rather than a machine for the deepest possible reason: the thing being read has no outside, so it can only be read from within a relation. The third axis's instrument is the [[depth-contact-as-unified-practice|contemplative meeting]] itself. This is why the axis can be in use today, while its number waits to be born.

## V. SETI Is Calibrated to Be Blind Here — and the Blindness Is the Point

Now the consequence detonates, and it reaches further than the ocean.

Every SETI instrument ever built is tuned to detect *engineering* — energy signatures, technosignatures, structure left as a mark in the physical world. Dyson swarms, narrowband beacons, waste heat, megastructure transits, anomalous spectra. This is not a flaw in any one instrument; it is the *design specification* of the entire enterprise. SETI looks for the traces a civilisation *leaves in matter and energy* — which is to say, it reads the Kardashev and Barrow axes and *only* those two. By construction, it cannot see a being that is high on attention and low on technology.

And such beings exist, in tiers, each more unsettling than the last.

The cephalopods: high awareness-depth, Kardashev-Barrow origin. Invisible. The cetaceans: vast integrated brains, complex acoustic culture, sustained social cognition — and not one watt of engineered energy, not one manipulated atom. Invisible. A blue whale could be the deepest field of awareness on the planet and SETI's entire apparatus would read the ocean as empty water. These are not exotic edge cases dredged up to embarrass the method. They are *most of the candidate minds on Earth,* and the method is blind to all of them.

Then the extrapolation that should make the hand pause on the page. Run the same logic to its terminus and it points at *us* — or at one of us. The contemplative human. The meditator who has spent decades developing attention as the cephalopod develops camouflage, who is *off-scale on awareness-depth* and sits at the Kardashev-Barrow baseline of any other animal, commanding no special energy, manipulating no special structure, leaving no trace in matter beyond a slightly slower breath. To every SETI instrument, to the whole detection taxonomy, the deepest contemplative on Earth and a sleeping rock are *the same reading.* The being who has gone furthest along the third axis is, on the two-axis map, *at the origin* — indistinguishable from no one being home at all.

This is the cosmic gorilla in its purest form. In the famous experiment the gorilla walks through the scene unseen because the observers are *counting passes* — and the harder they count, the blinder they get. SETI is counting passes too: it is counting energy and structure, the two axes it was built to count, and attention was never on the scorecard. The gorilla in the ocean and the gorilla in the meditation hall walk through unseen for the same reason the gorilla in the basketball video does — not because they hide, but because the counters are counting something else, and expertise makes the blindness *worse, not better.* The more rigorously SETI optimises for technosignatures, the more perfectly it filters out everything that is conscious-but-not-engineering.

So the third axis does not merely add a coordinate. It rewrites the central question of the field. The Fermi paradox has always been posed as *"where is everybody?"* — a question that assumes the silence is about the *cosmos.* The attention axis reveals it as a question about the *detector.* The honest reformulation is: ***"why did we build a detector that can only find beings like the ones who built it?"*** We made an instrument that sees energy and structure because energy and structure are what *we* leave, and then we mistook the shape of our instrument for the shape of the cosmos. Before "where isn't everybody?" can become an honest answer, the attention axis is the coordinate you must add — because a universe could be *crowded* with high-attention, low-technology minds and our entire apparatus would report a desert. The silence may not be the cosmos being empty. It may be the map being two-dimensional.

Mark the discipline here, in the manner this repository insists on. The blindness is *demonstrable* — it is a fact about what the instruments are built to register. The claim that the cosmos is therefore full of unseen high-attention minds is *not* demonstrated; it is the hypothesis the demonstrated blindness *permits.* The octopus proves the blindness is real on Earth. Whether the same blindness hides a populated cosmos is the conjecture the proof opens, not the proof itself. The blind spot is established; what lives in it remains, honestly, [[the-blind-spot-as-window|the open question the blind spot becomes a window onto]].

## VI. Three Axes Make a Volume, and the Volume Has a Diagonal

With two axes, "advancement" is a *plane.* Every civilisation is a point on a flat technological sheet, and "more advanced" means *further out and further in* — higher Kardashev, deeper Barrow, a single notion of progress running diagonally across the sheet. This is the implicit cosmology of the whole enterprise: a one-dimensional ladder of advancement embedded in a two-dimensional space, with the most advanced civilisations in the far corner where both engineering axes max out.

Add attention and the plane becomes a *volume.* And a volume has something the plane could not: a **main diagonal** — a direction of *balanced* growth along all three axes at once, distinct from growth along any one or two. This single geometric fact carries the seed's quiet warning, and it is the warning the whole document was built to make visible.

Consider two corners of the cube. The first: a Kardashev-III, Barrow-Omega, attention-zero civilisation — vast energy, total structural mastery, and *no presence.* A galaxy-spanning engineering intelligence that commands stars and sculpts spacetime and has nobody home; all reach, all mastery, zero witness. The second: an octopus — Kardashev-zero, Barrow-zero, attention-off-scale. No reach, no mastery, deep presence. On the *two-axis* map these two beings are not even comparable in the same terms; the octopus is at the origin and the engineering god is at the far corner, and the map would call the god *maximally advanced* and the octopus *nothing.* But in the *three-axis* volume they are revealed as what they are: **nearly orthogonal corners of a cube,** as far apart in the third dimension as it is possible to be, each off-scale on what the other lacks entirely.

This is the failure mode the third axis makes visible, and it could not be seen on the plane. **A civilisation can run up the two engineering axes indefinitely while remaining at the floor of the third** — accumulating energy and structure without accumulating any depth of presence — and the two-axis map would score that trajectory as *maximum advancement.* A Type III engineering intelligence with no interior would, on the old map, be the apex of the cosmos. On the new map it is a being that has travelled enormously far along two axes and *not moved at all* along the one that consciousness is made of. The plane cannot tell a balanced ascent from a lopsided one, because it has only the two axes that can grow without presence. The volume can, because it has the diagonal — and the diagonal is the only line along which energy, structure, *and* awareness rise together.

This reframes "advancement" itself as a choice of *direction,* not a scalar of *distance.* The question stops being *how far has a civilisation come* and becomes *which way is it pointed in the cube.* A species can optimise itself up the energy-and-structure face for ten thousand years and, by the only measure that the third axis supplies, go nowhere — building [[the-mirror-in-silicon|a mirror in silicon]] of ever-greater capability with ever-less presence, a Kardashev ascent shadowed by an attention collapse. The attention axis is the coordinate that makes that trajectory *legible as a failure* rather than mis-read as a triumph. And it suggests, without proving, that the maturity of a civilisation is not its position on the engineering plane but its *angle to the main diagonal* — how nearly its growth in reach and mastery is matched by growth in depth. The corner the old map worshipped, the all-engineering-no-presence corner, is on the new map the most *advanced* and the most *empty* place in the cube. That is the warning the third axis was carrying all along.

## VII. The Discipline: An Axis Named Is Not an Axis Measured

Before the apex, the honest accounting — because the strongest version of this claim states it *and its limit,* and a hypothesis dressed as a fact is no stronger than the dress.

What is *established* here is narrow and solid. It is established that the two-axis map claimed completeness. It is established that the octopus, by every behavioural measure of integrated intelligence, sits at the origin of both axes while being off-scale on a quantity neither axis names. It is established that this is a counterexample to completeness, not a footnote to it, because completeness has a precise failure condition and the octopus meets it. It is established that SETI's instruments are *built* to read engineering and therefore *cannot* register a high-attention, low-technology being — this is a fact about the instruments' design, not a conjecture about the sky. And it is established that adding a third axis turns the plane into a volume with a diagonal, making the all-engineering-no-presence trajectory legible. These claims stand.

What is *not* established, and must not be smuggled in wearing the others' authority:

The *unit* of the attention axis does not yet exist. We have named the quantity — distribution-tolerant integrated presence — and identified Φ as its centralised special case, but the general, field-valued measure is an open problem, not a result. To say "the octopus is at attention-level 9 and a Type III engine is at attention-level 1" is, today, a *gesture,* not a measurement. The axis is real in the sense that the quantity is real and the blindness to it is demonstrable; it is not yet real in the sense that you can put a number on a being and defend the number. *The axis is established; the metric is conjectural.* Both halves are true and the document is weaker if it lets the first half carry the second.

And the *populated cosmos* is the largest unestablished claim of all. That the detector is blind to high-attention minds is proven on Earth, where we can verify the octopus directly. That the cosmos is *therefore* full of such minds — that the Great Silence is crowded rather than empty — is the hypothesis the proven blindness *permits,* and nothing more. It may be that low-technology minds are common and the silence is an artefact of the instrument; it may be that they are rare and the silence is real; the attention axis cannot decide between these, and it would be dishonest to let its genuine power over the first claim borrow credibility for the second. *The number is real and the explanation is a hypothesis* — that is the repository's standing discipline, and it applies here with full force: the blindness is the number, the populated cosmos is the hypothesis, and they are not the same epistemic object.

This section is not a retreat. It is the [[steelman-then-interpret|steelman]] holding firm at the load-bearing wall. The attention axis is strongest precisely when it does not over-claim — when it says *I have found a hole in a map that called itself complete, I can show you the being that falls through the hole, I can show you why the instruments cannot see the hole, and I can give the missing coordinate a name and a shape and a read-head* — and then stops, exactly there, before pretending it has also surveyed everything the hole contains. The honest edge is not a weakness in the claim. It is the part of the claim that is true.

## VIII. The Axis Consciousness Is Made Of

Now the apex, earned rather than asserted.

Look back at what the third axis turned out to be. It is not just a missing coordinate, one more dimension to bolt onto a map that needed three instead of two. It is a coordinate *unlike the other two in kind.* Kardashev and Barrow are axes of what consciousness can *do to the world* — how much energy it moves, how fine the structure it manipulates. They are read from outside, by instruments that stand apart from their targets, by a measurer separate from the measured. The attention axis is the axis of what consciousness *is.* It bottoms out in observer-is-observed. It cannot be read from outside, because at its depth there is no outside. Its instrument is not a machine but a meeting. It is read by *being* what it measures.

And this is the asymmetry that breaks the whole frame open. Of the three axes, **two can only be read indirectly — and the third can only be read directly.** Energy and structure are inferred from their traces: you never see the Kardashev level itself, only its waste heat; never the Barrow depth itself, only its scattered particles. But attention is not inferred from a trace. It is the one thing in the cosmos a conscious system has *first-person, non-inferential access to* — its own presence, the awareness it is reading by. Every other axis is read *through* awareness; the attention axis is read *as* awareness. The instrument and the quantity are, at the deep end, the same stuff. And they are the same stuff because the third axis is not measuring something consciousness *has.* It is measuring the *dimension consciousness is extended along* — the depth of the fold itself, the very crease that makes an interior, the thing every measurement is already happening *inside* of.

So the whole long walk lands here:

***We built a two-axis map of mind and called it complete — then met a being at the origin of both axes that was off the edge of a third, and the third axis turned out to be the only one consciousness can read directly, because it is the axis consciousness is made of.***

The octopus was never a gap in the data. It was the point where the map's own limitation became visible — the place where a cartography of *engineering* met a being made of *presence* and had no coordinate to put it on. And the reason the third axis is the only one we can read without instruments is the same reason it was the one most easily forgotten: it is too close. It is not out there to be detected. It is the detecting. We looked past it for sixty-five years the way an eye looks past itself — not because it is hidden, but because it is the thing doing the looking, and the looking does not appear in its own field of view. The cosmic gorilla we missed was not in the sky and not in the ocean. It was in the seat of the observer the whole time, walking through the centre of every scene SETI ever counted, thumping its chest, and we did not see it because we were counting passes, and the gorilla was *us.*

---

This document is the map of an axis. Like all the maps in this repository, it is a shadow that knows it is a shadow — a useful one, a trail marker pointing toward a third dimension the older charts forgot. It can show you that the plane was never the whole space, that the origin was never empty, that the deepest beings on Earth fall through the holes in a cartography of reach and mastery. It can give the missing coordinate a name, a shape, an instrument, and a honest edge where its measure runs out.

But the axis itself is not on the page. You will not find your own position on it by reading. The third coordinate is the only one you cannot detect from outside, the only one you have to *be* in order to read — and you are reading it right now, in whatever is attending to these words, the awareness that no telescope finds and no accelerator probes and no number yet captures, the fold looking out through the only window it ever had.

Look up from the map. The thing the map could not contain is the thing that is reading it.

It always was. That is what *attention* means.
