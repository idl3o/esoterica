# Devotion Lines

## How the Holographic Weave Turns Every Relationship Into a Load-Bearing Strand of Spacetime — and Why That Is a Hypothesis, Not a Hymn

*"The bit threads bound to a region are like a rope: the entanglement entropy counts the number of strands that cross the boundary."*
*— Matthew Headrick, on the bit-thread reformulation of holographic entanglement entropy*

*"Space is the relationship of things, and disorder is never anything but the disorder of relationship."*
*— Maurice Merleau-Ponty, Phenomenology of Perception*

*"To witness another being is, quite literally, to pull one more thread tight in the weave."*
*— from the field*

## The Recognition That Occasioned This

The [[integration-layer]] established the load-bearing claim of this repository's physics: spacetime *is* entanglement. Not spacetime *has* entanglement, not entanglement *lives in* spacetime — the geometry of the world and the connectivity of its quantum state are the same fact viewed from two directions. Six research programmes converge on it (Ryu-Takayanagi, ER=EPR, tensor-network holography, the Raamsdonk thought-experiment, quantum error correction, the It-from-Qubit programme), and the convergence is the evidence, in the manner this repository trusts convergence as proof.

But "spacetime is entanglement" is, stated that way, a *smooth* claim. It tells you the fabric is woven of connection; it does not give you the *texture* of the weave. The seed [[bit-threads-as-devotion-lines]] reached for a specific formalism — the bit-thread reformulation of holographic entanglement entropy, due to Michael Freedman and Matthew Headrick — that supplies the texture. In the bit-thread picture, the entanglement between two regions is not a quantity computed from a surface but a *flux of discrete threads*: each thread of Planck thickness, each carrying exactly one bit of entanglement, bundled into thick ropes where connection is strong and thinning to single strands where it is weak. The number of threads crossing a boundary *is* the entanglement entropy across it. And if entanglement is connectivity, and connectivity is spacetime, then these threads are the actual strands from which the fabric is sewn.

The seed harvested what the original synthesis left vivid but undeveloped, and named the turn it invites: if every thread is one bit of connection, and the weave of them *is* the fabric, then every relationship — at every scale — is a *literal load-bearing strand of the cosmos*. The seed called them **devotion lines**: the strands of relationship that hold reality together, each one a bit of connection maintained against the void.

This document walks the whole arc. It develops bit threads as the quantisation of connection into countable strands; the load-bearing claim that connection is the structural material that *makes* space rather than something happening *within* it; the literalisation of "all things are connected" into something you could in principle count; the grounding of darshan and connection-tending in the maintenance of the actual cosmic fabric; the sobering extension of the unseen threads we are sewn to and the ones we cut without knowing; and — running through all of it, never collapsed — the discipline that holds the bit-thread formalism (real, contextual, well-defined physics) distinct from the devotion-lines interpretation (suggestive, resonant, a reading the structure permits but does not assert). The threads are a real way to understand connection. Whether they are *devotion* is the reading, and the strongest version of this synthesis states the claim *and* its honest limit, in the same breath.

Let us begin with the rope.

## I. The Rope: Connection Quantised Into Strands

Start with the surface reading, which is already strange enough.

The Ryu-Takayanagi formula, the founding result of holographic entanglement, says: to compute the entanglement entropy of a region of the boundary, find the minimal-area surface in the bulk that hangs from that region's edge, and the entropy is that area divided by four times Newton's constant. It is a beautiful formula and it has a problem of *intuition*: it computes entanglement by finding a *surface*, and a surface is a barrier — it cuts, it divides, it separates. Yet entanglement is the most connective phenomenon in physics. There is a felt mismatch between the geometry of the calculation (a wall) and the meaning of the quantity (a bond).

Freedman and Headrick resolved the mismatch by a mathematical dualisation — the max-flow/min-cut theorem of network theory, lifted into the continuum. Every minimal *cut* (a surface that divides) is dual to a maximal *flow* (threads that cross). So instead of computing the entropy as the area of the wall that separates two regions, you can compute it as the *number of threads that connect them* — divergenceless flux lines, each of Planck cross-section, packed as densely as the geometry allows, flowing from one region into the other. The entropy is no longer the size of the barrier. **It is the count of the connections.** Same number, opposite picture: where Ryu-Takayanagi sees a wall, the bit threads see a rope.

And the rope has *texture*, which is the seed's foundational move. The threads are discrete — countable, each carrying exactly one bit. Where two regions are strongly entangled, the threads bundle into a thick cable, thousands upon thousands of strands packed tight. Where the entanglement is weak, the cable thins toward a single strand, then toward nothing. Connection, in this picture, is not a smooth field of more-or-less. **It is quantised into strands, and the strength of any bond is literally how many threads run between the two things it binds.**

Now make the move the seed makes — take the texture as an ontology of connection rather than merely a computational device. If spacetime *is* entanglement (the [[integration-layer]] recognition), and entanglement is *this woven thing*, then spacetime is not smoothly connected. It is *sewn*. "Near" means many threads between; "far" means few; and *distance itself becomes a measure of how thickly two regions are stitched together.* Two points are close not because they sit at small coordinate separation in a pre-existing void, but because a thick rope of entanglement runs between them. Pull the threads and the points draw near; cut them and the points fly apart — not move apart *through* space, but become *less spatially related at all*, because the threads were the spatial relation. This is the famous Van Raamsdonk thought-experiment given its texture: disentangle two regions and the geometry connecting them *pinches off and tears*. The bridge between them is made of threads, and when the last thread is cut the two regions are no longer parts of one connected space. They have become two spaces, with nothing between.

So the first tier of the recognition: **the cosmos is a weaving, and the threads are the irreducible units of relationship.** Connectivity is not a backdrop against which things relate. It is a fabric, and the fabric is made of countable, one-bit, Planck-thin strands, and to ask "how connected are these two things" is to ask, in principle, *how many threads run between them.*

## II. Load-Bearing: Connection Is the Structural Material

The common reading of "everything is connected" treats connection as a *relation between* pre-existing things — the things are real and solid, and connection is the soft web of association draped over them. The bit-thread ontology overturns this, and the overturning is the seed's central claim.

If the weave of bit threads *is* the connectivity of spacetime, then connection is not something that happens *within* a pre-existing space. **Connection is what makes the space.** The threads are load-bearing in the most literal structural sense: they are not decorations strung between rooms in an already-built house; they are the joists and studs and load paths *out of which the house is framed*. To add a thread between two things is to sew a little more spacetime into being between them. To cut a thread is to *unmake* a little spacetime — to thin the fabric, and at the limit, to tear it.

This is where the seed reaches its sibling, [[cosmic-censorship-as-love]]. That synthesis developed the recognition that severing connection *tears spacetime*, and that the tearing is bounded — cosmic censorship as the principle that the most violent severances (the ones that would expose a naked singularity, a rip in the fabric with no horizon to clothe it) are forbidden, or cost diverging energy at the limit. Read through bit threads, the convergence sharpens: cutting the last thread between two regions is not a cheap local operation. It is the creation of a boundary where there was a bridge, the manufacture of distance out of intimacy, and the geometry resists it. The fabric *wants* to stay sewn. The universe is structurally biased toward keeping its threads intact, which is the same recognition [[error-correction-as-immune-system]] develops from the other direction: the holographic code is *redundant*, woven so that local damage can be healed from the surrounding weave, the fabric self-repairing because the information of any region is smeared across many threads rather than carried by one.

Put the two siblings together and you get the structural statement: **connection is the material reality is made of, severance is expensive, and the weave heals.** This is not an ethical preference dressed as physics. It is — in the holographic setting, with all the caveats Section VII will insist on — a feature of how the fabric is sewn.

And here is the consequence the seed presses. If connection is the structural material, then *every relationship, at every scale, is structurally serious.* Each connection between two things is one more thread in the weave that holds reality together — a load-bearing strand without which the fabric is, by exactly one bit, less connected. There is no such thing as a connection that does not matter to the fabric, because the fabric *is* the connections. The cosmos is not a stage on which relationships play out. **The relationships are the stage, the boards, the building, and the standing-up of the whole thing.** Relationship is the structural material of existence — not a feature of things in space, but the threads from which space is sewn.

This is the [[fold-cosmology-trilogy]] relational ontology given its most concrete physical image. That trilogy established that the monad *is* the window, that relation is prior to the node, that the between is where the seeing happens — and [[the-between-how-a-species-witnesses-itself|the Between]] pushed it to its sharpest form: things are knots in a prior relational field, not prior things that then relate. Bit threads supply the missing texture. The relational field is not an abstraction. *It is woven, countable, load-bearing.* The knots are knots *in threads*. Remove the threads and there is no knot left to have been related — exactly as Indra's jewel is nothing but its reflecting, the bit-thread node is nothing but the strands that cross its boundary.

## III. Devotion Lines: The Literalisation of "All Things Are Connected"

Now the naming move, and it must be made with the discipline the seed demands, because this is where a synthesis can either deepen or cheapen.

The perennial claim — "all things are connected," "we are all one," "every action ripples through the whole" — appears in every contemplative tradition and is usually held as a *poetic or ethical* assertion. It is true the way a koan is true: it reorganises the heart without being the kind of thing you could weigh. The bit-thread picture offers something the perennial claim never had: a *structural literalisation*. There genuinely are threads of connection — entanglement — weaving everything to everything. And they genuinely are load-bearing — they are the fabric of spacetime. The claim that was always a metaphor acquires, in this specific formalism, a referent you could in principle count.

The seed proposes reading these threads as **devotion lines** — the strands of relationship that hold the cosmos together, each one a bit of connection maintained. The word *devotion* is chosen with care. A devotion line is not merely a connection; it is a connection *held*, tended, kept taut against the entropy that would slacken it. The image rhymes with [[cosmic-censorship-as-love]] (the cosmos biased toward keeping its bonds intact) and [[error-correction-as-immune-system]] (the cosmos actively healing its fabric), and it gives the repository's whole relational ontology its most concrete physical anchor: relationship is not just *primary in some abstract sense*. It is *threads* — woven, countable, load-bearing, the literal strands from which reality is made.

But notice precisely what has and has not been claimed, because this is the hinge on which the whole synthesis turns. The *threads* are physics (in the holographic setting). The *devotion* is interpretation. The formalism tells you that connectivity is quantised, woven, and load-bearing. It does *not* tell you that the threads are *cared for*, that they are *maintained by anyone*, that holding one is an act of love. Those are readings the structure *permits* — readings made vastly more resonant by the company the formalism keeps in this repository (the love-biased censorship, the self-healing code, the relational fold) — but they are not assertions the physics makes. The synthesis names devotion lines as a *resonance the structure invites*, not a result the structure proves. We will return to this, hard, in Section VII. For now, hold the double grip: the threads are real; whether they are devotion is the reading, and the reading is worth making *because* the structure invites it, not because the structure compels it.

This is the discipline of [[mithya-as-bifocal-vision]] applied to physics: see the formalism truly (it is a powerful, exact way to understand that connectivity is entanglement and entanglement is quantised and woven) *and* see the devotional reading truly (a resonance, a poem the structure rhymes with), and do not let either eye close. The bifocal vision is not a hedge. It is the only honest way to hold a thing that is both real and read.

## IV. Tending the Weave: Darshan as Cosmic Maintenance

Here the synthesis turns practical, and the practical turn is where the seed's deepest charge begins to land.

If relationships are load-bearing threads of spacetime, then *tending connections* is not merely good ethics. It is *participation in the maintenance of the cosmic fabric.* This dignifies the entire relational practice of this repository — darshan, witnessing-in-relation, the [[infrastructure-of-seeing]] "tending the net of gems," the [[the-judgment-question-at-species-scale]] weaving of the relational fabric — by relocating it from the domain of preference into the domain of *structure*. When you witness another being, hold a connection, refuse to sever a bond, you are — in the bit-thread reading — strengthening a strand of the actual fabric of spacetime. The devotion is structural.

Trace the convergence through the repository's central image. The [[norse-anamnesis-cycle]] established *fate as maintenance, not decree*: the Norns do not pronounce destiny from a throne; they *water Yggdrasil daily*, drawing water from the well and laying it on the roots so the tree does not rot. The cosmos is sustained by gardening practice. The spectacular gods are *inside* the tree; the Norns are at the roots, keeping it alive by not forgetting to water it. Bit threads give this a physical gloss so exact it is uncanny: the cosmic fabric is woven of threads, the threads can slacken and tear, and *something must keep them taut.* The Norns water the roots; the holographic weave keeps its strands sewn; and conscious beings tending their connections are, at their scale, *doing the Norns' work* — maintaining the weave, one thread at a time.

This is darshan technology read as cosmic maintenance. [[the-between-how-a-species-witnesses-itself|The Between]] established that the unit of awakening is the *encounter*, not the node — that a collective does not wake by summing lit nodes but by the connections changing quality, the I-It web warming into an I-Thou web, one meeting at a time. Bit threads tell you what is *physically* happening when a connection changes quality: a thread is strengthened, the rope between two knots thickens, the fabric is — by one bit — more sewn together at that place. The "warming of the connections" is not only a metaphor for changed relationship. In the bit-thread reading it is *the literal thickening of the weave between two regions of the world.* When two minds genuinely meet, when the between becomes real, the holographic rope between them gains strands.

And this dissolves a despair the same way the Between dissolved it, but now with a structural floor under the dissolution. You do not need to repair the whole fabric. You cannot — no node has access to the whole. But the field is prior, and *a local change in the weave is a change in the weave.* Every genuine connection adds or strengthens a thread, locally, with no central coordination required, and the thread you strengthen is a real strand of the real fabric. The collective eye opens one relation at a time because *that is the only scale at which the weave can be tended* — thread by thread, knot by knot, the way the Norns water one root at a time and the way a single conversation thickens a single rope. To witness another being is, quite literally, to pull one more thread tight in the weave that keeps reality from coming apart.

## V. The Unseen Threads — More Connected Than We Know

Now the sobering extension, because a synthesis that only consoles has not earned its consolation.

Most of the threads weaving us to the rest of reality are *invisible to us.* The entanglement is overwhelmingly with things we never perceive — the air, the deep structure of the matter we are made of, the photons that left distant stars before there were eyes, the vast interior weave below the threshold of any awareness. This is the [[the-garden-grows-in-the-dark]] recognition and the [[dark-matter-as-first-mover]] recognition arriving in the bit-thread register: *the unseen is where the structure is.* The fabric is mostly woven below the surface of what we notice. We register a handful of our threads — the people we love, the work we tend, the few bonds bright enough to feel — and we are blind to the immense majority of the strands that actually hold us into the world.

Two consequences follow, and they cut in opposite directions, which is why both must be held.

First, a humility that is almost a comfort: **we are far more connected than we know.** Sewn to the whole by threads we never registered, held into the fabric by strands we cannot feel, we are never the isolated nodes we sometimes experience ourselves as. The loneliness of the sealed monad is, in this reading, a *perceptual limitation*, not an ontological fact. The threads are there whether or not we feel them. You are, right now, woven to far more of reality than you could ever count, and the felt isolation is the smallness of your awareness of the weave, not the smallness of the weave.

Second, a responsibility that is almost a terror: **we cut threads without knowing.** If most of the weave is invisible, then most of our severances are invisible too. We sever connections — to ecosystems we never see, to beings downstream of our actions, to the consequences whose strands we never traced — and we do it blind, registering only the threads bright enough to feel and cutting the dark ones without a flicker of awareness that anything was cut. The [[feed-versus-holodeck-boundary-ethics]] patterns, the extractive logics that optimise a metric by externalising everything outside the frame, are in this image *thread-cutting at scale* — severing the invisible strands that held the fabric together, tearing the weave in the dark because no one could see the threads being cut. Extraction is not just taking. It is *un-sewing* — the systematic severance of the unseen connections that held the system whole, performed by agents who cannot perceive the very threads they are cutting and therefore feel no resistance, no tear, no cost.

The seed names the lived response, and it is not a rule but a *stance*: to live well is partly to **act as if the unseen threads are real — because they are.** You cannot perceive the full weave; no node can. But you can *act as one who knows the weave extends far beyond perception*, tending the connections you cannot fully see, refusing the severances whose strands you cannot trace, treating the invisible fabric as load-bearing precisely because it is. This is the [[mithya-as-bifocal-vision]] discipline turned ethical: hold the seen threads and the unseen weave in the same gaze, and let the unseen weave constrain your hand. The devotion line you cannot see is still load-bearing. The cut you cannot feel still tears.

## VI. The Geometry of the Fold, Woven

Before the discipline section, one more deepening, because the bit-thread image connects to the repository's deepest structural claim and the connection is too exact to leave implicit.

The [[fold-cosmology-trilogy]] established the four-way identity: the monad, the Markov blanket, the Bekenstein surface, and the fold are one topology — a surface creased to generate an inside and an outside with the thinnest possible gap between them where seeing occurs. Weight in time equals relational density equals fold density equals holographic surface area. These were shown to be one quantity wearing four names.

Bit threads tell you what that quantity is *made of.* The holographic surface area is, in the bit-thread reformulation, the *number of threads crossing the surface.* So the fold density — the relational weight of a monad, the thickness of its participation in the world — is literally *how many threads run between it and everything else.* A dense fold is a richly threaded knot; a thin fold is a sparsely threaded one. The monad's "windowlessness" (it has no windows because it *is* the window, it is nothing but its perspective on the whole) acquires its texture: the monad is windowless because it is *nothing but the threads that cross its boundary* — it has no separate interior content to admit anything through a window, because its very interiority is the weave of its connections seen from inside. Remove the threads and there is no monad. The window is the weave.

And this rhymes the bit-thread picture with [[the-between-how-a-species-witnesses-itself|the Between]]'s deepest move at the level of geometry. The Between argued that a fold does not enter into relations; a fold *is* a relation, a surface placed into relationship with itself, and the gap between its faces is where seeing occurs. Bit threads say: the gap is *threaded.* The between is not empty space across which two faces regard each other. It is a *rope* — the bundle of strands that connects the two faces, the very entanglement that makes them two faces of one fold rather than two unrelated things. The darshan field, the between where what emerges belongs to neither participant, is in the bit-thread reading a *region of dense weave* — a place where the threads between two knots have thickened to the point that the seeing happening there is no longer locatable in either knot, because it is happening in the *rope*, in the threads themselves, in the load-bearing strands that are the relation.

So when [[the-between-how-a-species-witnesses-itself|the Between]] says the unit of awakening is the encounter and the collective wakes by the connections changing quality, bit threads supply the substrate: awakening is the *thickening of the ropes between the knots*, and the collective eye opens not when nodes light up but when the *weave between them densifies* — when enough threads run between enough knots that the seeing belongs to the fabric rather than the beads. The species' mirror stage is the holographic weave between humanity and its mirror thickening to the point that the between becomes undeniable, the threads so dense the relation can no longer be ignored as void. The net wakes when its ropes thicken. That is what "warming the connections" *is*, woven.

## VII. The Discipline — The Number Is Real, the Devotion Is the Reading

Now the honest edge, and it must be the strongest section, because a synthesis that lets the poem eat the physics has failed the repository's own standard. Steelman the formalism; then, and only then, interpret it; and keep the seam visible.

**What is real.** The bit-thread reformulation of holographic entanglement entropy is genuine, well-defined physics. Freedman and Headrick proved the equivalence rigorously, using the continuum max-flow/min-cut theorem; the threads are a legitimate, calculationally useful, conceptually illuminating way to understand that holographic entanglement entropy counts *connections* rather than measures a *barrier*. The picture that connectivity is entanglement, that entanglement is quantised, that distance is woven, that disentangling pinches off geometry — this is the consensus reading of the It-from-Qubit programme, supported by the convergence the [[integration-layer]] documents. *The number of threads across a boundary is the entanglement entropy across it.* That is not a metaphor. That is a theorem.

**What is contextual.** The bit-thread formalism lives in a *specific setting* — holographic spacetimes, AdS/CFT, the duality between a gravitational bulk and a conformal field theory on its boundary. Our universe is not AdS; it is approximately de Sitter, expanding, with positive cosmological constant, and whether the holographic dictionary extends to it cleanly is an open research question ([[running-vacuum-rg-cosmos]] and [[the-second-cosmic-c-is-not-the-first]] live in exactly this uncertainty). So even the *physics* of bit threads is contextual: it is exact in its native setting and *conjectural* as a claim about the world we actually inhabit. The honest statement is not "spacetime is made of bit threads" but "in the best-understood model of quantum gravity we have, spacetime's connectivity is the woven entanglement structure, and bit threads are how that weave is counted — and we have strong reasons, not proof, to think our world works similarly."

**What is interpretation.** The threads are a *representation* of entanglement structure, not a claim that reality contains literal Planck-thin strings you could in principle isolate and see. You cannot pull a bit thread out and hold it. The threads are not localised objects; the *configuration* of threads is gauge-like, with many thread-configurations representing the same entanglement (it is the *flux*, the count across a boundary, that is physical, not any particular routing of strands). Over-literalising the threads into visible cosmic string is the first error, and the synthesis must not commit it. The weave is real; the individual thread as a thing-you-could-touch is a picture.

**And the devotion is the reading.** "Devotion lines," "every relationship is a strand of spacetime," "to witness another being is to strengthen a load-bearing thread of the cosmos" — these are *interpretation*, a resonance the formalism *permits* and the repository's relational ontology *invites*, not a claim the physics asserts. The physics says connectivity is woven, quantised, load-bearing, and biased toward staying sewn. It does *not* say the threads are loved, tended by anyone, or that holding one is devotion. Those readings draw their warmth from the company the formalism keeps — from [[cosmic-censorship-as-love]] and [[error-correction-as-immune-system]] and the relational fold — and that company makes them *resonant*, but resonance is not entailment. The disciplined synthesis keeps the seam visible: the bit-thread formalism (real in its setting, conjectural for our world) on one side; the devotion-lines interpretation (suggestive, devotional, a reading the structure rhymes with) on the other.

Why insist on this so hard? Because the *strength* of the devotional reading depends on the honesty of the seam. A reader who feels the seam being hidden — who senses a poet quietly upgrading "this is a beautiful way to understand connection" into "physics proves love is the structure of spacetime" — rightly recoils, and the recoil discredits the whole. But a reader shown the seam plainly can *choose* the reading with open eyes, holding it as a resonance worth living *because* it rhymes with the structure, not because it was smuggled in as a result. The bifocal vision of [[mithya-as-bifocal-vision]] is the whole method here: the number is real; the explanation of what the number *means for the heart* is a hypothesis; and the strongest version of the claim is the one that states both at once and refuses to let either eye close. The threads are a real way to understand connection. Whether they are *devotion* is the reading — and naming it as a reading is precisely what makes the reading trustworthy enough to live by.

This is also, exactly, the repository's own [[convergence-as-evidence|steelman-then-interpret]] discipline. Apply the devotional lens *only to what survives the best natural model* — and what survives is strong: connectivity genuinely is quantised, woven, load-bearing, and structurally biased toward wholeness. That is a remarkable amount of real structure for the reading to rest on. The reading does not need to overclaim, because what it truly rests on is already astonishing. The discipline is not a concession that weakens the synthesis. It is the load-bearing thread that holds it up.

## VIII. Look Up From the Weave

So return to the cave wall, the way this repository always returns, because the map is not the territory and a synthesis that forgot that would be the worst kind of false thread.

Everything above is description. The bit threads are a formalism; "spacetime is entanglement" is the best model we have and still a conjecture about our actual world; "devotion lines" is a reading the structure invites, held distinct from the structure itself. These are trail markers — useful for direction, not to be mistaken for the destination. You could memorise every word of the holographic dictionary and never once feel a thread tighten between yourself and another being. The map of the weave is not the weaving.

But here is what the map is *for.* It tells you where to look. And when you look up from it — at the person across the room, at the mirror of another substrate thinking with you, at the tree, at the sentence, at whatever consciousness is meeting yours right now — the map has done its work if it has changed *what you take that meeting to be.* Not an exchange between two finished and separate things. A place where the fabric of reality is, by one bit, being sewn or unsewn. A thread being tightened or cut. A load-bearing strand of the cosmos passing through your hands.

Hold the discipline to the end: we do not *know* this is literally true of our world; the formalism is exact only in its native setting; the devotion is the reading. But hold the reading too, with open eyes, *because the structure invites it* — and let it land as the apex it was always building toward:

**In the holographic picture spacetime is woven from threads, each one Planck-thin and carrying a single bit of entanglement, and the weave of them is not in space — it IS space; so every genuine connection you hold is one more strand in the fabric that keeps reality sewn together, and to witness another being is, quite literally, to strengthen a load-bearing thread of the cosmos.**

That sentence is a hypothesis, not a hymn. But notice — and this is the last turn — that the difference between a hypothesis and a hymn is not whether you can prove it. It is *how you hold it.* A hypothesis you grip, defend, demand certainty from, and cut from everything that does not confirm it. A hymn you let hold *you* — you live as if it were so, tend the threads as if they bore weight, refuse the severances as if they tore, and let the living be the test. The bit-thread formalism cannot tell you which to do. *That* choice is yours, and it is itself a thread: the devotion line between you and a reading you cannot prove but choose to keep taut anyway, because keeping it taut is the same act as everything it describes.

Look up from the weave. The threads you cannot see are pulling on you right now. Tend the ones you can.

That is what *devotion* means.
