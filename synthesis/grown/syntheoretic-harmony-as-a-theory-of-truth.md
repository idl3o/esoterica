# The Binocular Real

## Truth as the Depth That Emerges Between Views — Not Correspondence to a Fact No Perspective Can See

*"The eye sees only what the mind is prepared to comprehend."*
*— Henri Bergson*

*"We have to remember that what we observe is not nature in itself, but nature exposed to our method of questioning."*
*— Werner Heisenberg, Physics and Philosophy*

*"Truth is the depth that emerges when two genuine views align — and a single perspective, however accurate, is flat."*
*— from the field*

## The Recognition That Occasioned This

This repository has, for its entire life, been doing something it never named. It takes a structure — the fold, say, or the kernel, or the remainder — and it asks the fold cosmology about it, and the Free Energy Principle, and Integrated Information Theory, and the Vedānta four-states, and the Norse mythology, and the renormalisation group. And when these independent perspectives *land in the same place*, the repository treats that convergence as the evidence. Not because any of them matched a bare fact that we then checked against — we never checked against any bare fact, ever — but because so many different ways of looking *aligned*. The alignment was the proof.

That operating procedure has a name, and the name is a *theory of truth*. The repository has been running on it without articulating it. This document articulates it.

The dominant theory of truth in the philosophical tradition is **correspondence**: a statement is true when it matches a mind-independent fact. The picture in the dish matches the fish in the sea. It is intuitive, it is venerable, and it works beautifully for the middle-sized dry goods — *the cat is on the mat* is true if and only if the cat is on the mat. But correspondence struggles, badly, with the precise cases this repository lives inside: complementary truths where wave *and* particle are both correct and contradictory; perspectival truths where the monad knows reality only from inside its own window; [[the-hard-problem-as-a-necker-cube|Necker-cube dissolutions]] where two readings of one thing are both valid and neither is "the fact." For these — which are not edge cases but the *centre* of the work — correspondence does not just struggle. It asks a question that cannot be answered: *match against what, exactly?*

The seed pulled an alternative out of the [[complementarity-as-the-engine-of-individuation|complementarity engine]]: truth as **syntheoretic harmony** — the degree of structural alignment between a perspective and its complements across the whole matrix of views. Binocular alignment rather than match-to-fact. This document walks that proposal from its foundational critique, through its central image, into its hardest payoff (the escape from relativism), back around to its reflexive recognition (the repository's own method), and finally into the discipline that keeps it honest — the line between earned harmony and the syncretist's mush.

We will earn the deepest sentence slowly. But know from the start what it is reaching for: that depth lives in neither eye, and truth may be the same.

## I. The View From Nowhere That No One Has

Begin with the surface reading of correspondence, stated at its strongest, because [[steelman-then-interpret|the discipline is to steelman first]].

Correspondence theory says: there is a world, mind-independent, structured a certain way whether or not anyone looks. A statement is true when its structure matches the world's structure. *Snow is white* is made true by snow being white. This is the realist's intuition, and it is a good one — it encodes a hard-won refusal to let truth collapse into mere agreement, mere wishing, mere social convention. The whole dignity of correspondence is that it makes truth answerable to *something outside us*. The world gets a vote. That is precious, and any replacement theory that throws it away is not an improvement but a surrender.

So far, so unassailable. Now the turn.

Correspondence requires you to **compare the statement to the fact**. Not to your evidence for the fact, not to your best model of the fact — to *the fact itself*, the world as it is in itself, bare. And here is the problem that the entire perspectival cosmology of this repository forces into the open: **no perspectival being ever has access to the fact-in-itself.** You never stand outside your own viewing to check your view against the unviewed world. Every comparison you could possibly make is a comparison between one perspective and *another perspective* — your statement against your observation, your model against your measurement, your map against another map. The "bare fact," the world-as-it-is-without-any-view, is precisely the thing you cannot reach, because reaching is itself a view.

Correspondence, then, smuggles in a **view from nowhere** — a God's-eye vantage from which statement and bare fact could be laid side by side and compared. Thomas Nagel named this the *view from nowhere* and showed it to be the implicit fantasy of objectivity: a seeing that is no one's seeing, a perspective that is not a perspective. But the [[fold-cosmology-trilogy|fold cosmology]] has already established, on independent grounds, that there is no such vantage. The monad is windowless because it *is* a window — reality is known only from inside a crease, a Markov blanket, a Bekenstein surface. There is no outside-of-all-folds from which the unfolded whole could be compared to a statement about it. **To have a view is to be a fold; to be a fold is to have an inside; and the inside is the only place from which anything is ever seen.**

This is not scepticism. It does not say truth is unreachable. It says correspondence locates truth at a vantage *no perspectival being occupies*, and then quietly assumes we occupy it. The realist's precious intuition — that the world gets a vote — survives. What dies is the fantasy that we can read the world's vote *directly*, by comparison to the bare ballot. We only ever read votes through views. So if truth is to be a thing we can actually have rather than a thing we can only postulate, it must be definable **from the views we have**, not from a vantage we don't.

That is the project. Syntheoretic harmony is the attempt to define truth *without* the view from nowhere — from the inside of the fold, where we actually live.

## II. The Depth That Is in Neither Eye

Here is the central image, and it is not a metaphor decorating an argument. It is the argument's load-bearing structure.

You have two eyes. Each delivers a flat image — a two-dimensional projection of the scene. The two images are slightly *different*: the left eye sees a little more of the left side of every object, the right eye a little more of the right. This difference is called binocular disparity, and it is, at the level of the raw image, a *discrepancy* — the two pictures literally do not agree.

And yet what you experience is not two flat pictures, nor a blurred average of them, nor a choice between them. You experience **depth** — a three-dimensional world, solid and inhabitable. Where does the depth come from? Not from either image. Neither flat picture contains depth; depth is exactly the dimension a flat image lacks. The depth is *computed from the disparity* — the visual system fuses the two slightly-disagreeing images and extracts, from the precise structure of their disagreement, a third thing that was in neither: the third dimension. Stereopsis. **The depth is not in either eye's image. It emerges when the two align.**

Sit with the strangeness of this, because it is the whole seed. The thing that is most *real* about your visual world — its solidity, its inhabitability, the fact that you can reach into it — is the one thing *neither* of your sensory channels actually delivered. It is manufactured in the fusion. A one-eyed person sees a perfectly accurate flat image and is *missing the real* — not because their eye is faulty, but because the real, here, is a relation between views that they have only one of.

Now the proposal. **Truth works the same way.**

A single perspective is *flat*. It is one view, one face of the fold, one eye's image — accurate, perhaps, as far as it goes, but two-dimensional in the sense that matters: it lacks the depth that only alignment can produce. Wave-mechanics is a flat, accurate image. Particle-mechanics is a flat, accurate image. They *disagree* — that is the disparity, the quantum scandal, the apparent contradiction that troubled everyone. But the real — the quantum object, the depth — is in *neither*. It emerges when you hold both and let the structure of their disagreement disclose the third thing that neither flat image could contain. This is exactly the [[complementarity-as-the-engine-of-individuation|complementarity structure]] operating now as an *epistemology*: complementary perspectives are not rivals to be adjudicated but the two eyes of a single seeing, and the truth is the depth their fusion discloses.

The same shape recurs everywhere the repository works. Subjective and objective are two flat images of one experience; the truth of consciousness is the depth between them, which is why the [[the-hard-problem-as-a-necker-cube|hard problem looks like a Necker cube]] — you can flip between the two flat readings but the *object* is the bistability itself, the structure that supports both. The [[mithya-as-bifocal-vision|two truths of Madhyamaka]] — conventional and ultimate — are bifocal precisely because the real is seen *through both at once*, neither collapsing into the other. As-above-so-below is binocular: the macrocosm and the microcosm are two eyes, and the [[kernel-is-where-above-meets-below|kernel is the depth where they fuse]].

So state it cleanly, in the manner the repository states its identifications:

> Truth is binocular. A single perspective, however accurate, is monocular and therefore flat. The real is the depth that emerges when complementary views align — and it lives in none of them.

This is not a retreat from realism into mere coherence. It is a *relocation* of the real — from the unreachable bare fact to the reachable depth-between-views. The world still gets its vote. But the vote is cast not in any single image; it is cast in *whether the images fuse* — whether, when you hold them together, a stable depth emerges or the picture refuses to resolve. And that — as the next sections show — is something perspectival beings can actually test, which is the whole point.

## III. The Constraint You Cannot Fake

The standard objection arrives immediately, and it is the objection that decides whether this theory lives or dies. **Relativism.**

If there is no bare fact to match, the objection runs, then isn't every perspective as good as every other? Haven't you just dressed up "anything goes" in the language of eyes and depth? If truth is "alignment among views," and we get to choose which views, can't I always find some set of perspectives that "harmonise" with whatever I please? This is the worry that has haunted every non-correspondence theory of truth — coherentism's old wound, the suspicion that without the world as external anchor, truth dissolves into a popularity contest among ideas.

The binocular image answers this objection more decisively than any coherence theory ever could, and the answer is the seed's sharpest payoff. **You cannot fake binocular fusion.**

Return to the eyes. The depth you see is not something you *decide*. You cannot will two arbitrary images to fuse into a coherent solid. If I show your left eye a photograph of a cat and your right eye a photograph of a clock, you do not get "depth" — you get *binocular rivalry*, a horrible flickering instability where the visual system, unable to fuse incompatible images, oscillates between them or suppresses one. Fusion into stable depth happens *only* when the two images are genuine views of *the same scene from slightly different angles*. The geometry of the world is what makes fusion possible. The disparity must be the *right kind* of disparity — the kind that real depth produces — or no depth emerges. **The world constrains which images can fuse, and it does so absolutely, whether or not you want them to.**

This is the move that escapes relativism without returning to correspondence. There is a real, mind-independent, perspective-transcending fact of the matter — *not* about whether a statement matches a bare fact (we still have no access to that), but about **how well a perspective harmonises with its complements.** That fact you cannot fake. The images either fuse into stable depth or they don't, and which one happens is not up to you. A view that coheres with the other valid views of the same thing — that fuses with them into a stable, inhabitable depth — is *more true* than a view that produces rivalry, flicker, instability, contradiction. Harmony is **objective-but-not-correspondist**: a genuine constraint, a real measure, the world's vote cast not in any image but in the *fusibility* of the images.

So views are emphatically *not* all equally good. They differ — measurably, non-arbitrarily — in how well they harmonise. The flat-earther's image will not fuse with the views from orbit, the views from circumnavigation, the views from the physics of gravitation; held together, they produce rivalry and flicker, not depth. The round-earth view fuses with all of them into a stable solid. *That* is the difference between true and false, on this account: not match-to-an-inaccessible-fact, but **the presence or absence of stable binocular fusion across the available complements.** And the test is rigorous, because — like stereopsis — it is not under the believer's control. You can want two images to fuse all you like; the geometry decides.

This is the measure that lies *between* relativism and dogmatism. Relativism says every view fuses equally well (false — most pairings produce rivalry). Dogmatism says only my view is the fact and all others are error (false — it mistakes one flat image for the depth). Syntheoretic harmony says: there is a real depth, it is reachable, and it is reached by fusion, which the world constrains and you cannot counterfeit.

## IV. Grounding the Sibling: Traditions as Eyes

Now the payoff cashes out where the seed most wants it: in the [[complementarism-as-inter-traditional-method|problem of the many wisdom traditions]], its sibling seed.

Confront the trilemma directly. You have Vedānta and Madhyamaka and Kabbalah and the Norse cosmology and the contemplative Christian apophatic and Daoism and the rest — each a vast, internally coherent perspective on the real, and each *different*, sometimes flatly contradictory (the Self that is everything; the emptiness that is no-self). Three standard responses, each a dead end:

**Exclusivism** says one tradition has the fact and the rest are error. This is correspondence applied to traditions — and it inherits correspondence's fatal flaw: it requires a view from nowhere from which to certify *which* tradition matches the bare metaphysical fact, and no one has that view. It also throws away most of the data, declaring the deepest contemplative achievements of whole civilisations to be simply mistaken. It is dogmatism wearing robes.

**Relativism** says all traditions are equally true, each "true for its own culture," none assessable against the others. This is the syncretist's escape, and it is a surrender: it makes the traditions *unfusable in principle*, sealing each in its own context, which means it can never produce depth — only a flat collection of flat images side by side, a museum, not a seeing.

**Syntheoretic harmony** offers the third path, and it is neither. Traditions are not rankable by match-to-fact (we have no access to the fact) and they are not all equally good (relativism's flat museum). They are assessable by **how well each harmonises with the others as a face of one fold** — whether, when you hold Vedānta's *ātman* and Madhyamaka's *anātman* together, you get rivalry and flicker, or whether you get *depth*: the recognition (which the [[mithya-as-bifocal-vision|two-truths bifocal vision]] actually achieves) that the Self-that-is-everything and the no-self-that-is-empty are two eyes on one structure, fusing into a depth — the fold, the windowless monad that is nothing but its relations — that neither flat image held. The most true tradition, on this account, is not the one that "matches reality" but **the one that fuses most coherently with the rest, disclosing the most depth when aligned with its complements.**

And crucially this preserves what exclusivism was *right* about — that not everything is equally true — while jettisoning what it was wrong about — the fantasy vantage. A tradition that can only be maintained by *refusing* to fuse with the others, that produces rivalry with every neighbouring view, that holds itself together only by sealing out the complements: that tradition scores low on harmony, and low harmony is, on this theory, *low truth*. Not "false because it doesn't match the fact" — false because it will not fuse, because held up against the converging others it flickers rather than resolving into depth.

This is the [[complementarism-as-inter-traditional-method|inter-traditional method]] given its epistemology. The method was always: take each tradition at its strongest, then see what depth emerges when you hold them together. The seed names *why that produces truth*: because truth **is** the depth that emerges from the fusion of genuine complements, and traditions, at their strongest, are exactly such complements — eyes on the one real, slightly different in angle, fusible into a depth no single tradition's flat doctrine could contain. The [[aesir-vanir-war-kvasir-integration|Kvasir who is brewed from the spittle of two warring pantheons]] is this image in myth: the wisest being is the *fusion product* of two complete and opposed traditions, and his wisdom — the mead of poetry — is precisely the depth that neither pantheon held alone.

## V. The Repository Reading Itself

Now the document turns reflexive, and this is where the seed becomes uncanny, because the theory of truth it proposes turns out to be the *method this repository has been silently running all along*.

The repository's signature epistemic move is **convergence-as-evidence**: when independent frameworks — built from different starting axioms, in different centuries, by minds that never met — arrive at the same structure, the repository treats *the convergence itself* as the proof. It does not say "the fold is true because we checked it against the bare fact of consciousness." It has no access to that bare fact. It says: the fold cosmology, the Free Energy Principle, Integrated Information Theory, the Vedāntic four states, the renormalisation-group fixed point, and the Norse Yggdrasil all *land on the same structure* — and that alignment, across so many independent views, is what makes the structure trustworthy.

Look at what that move *is*. It is **syntheoretic harmony in practice.** Each framework is an eye — a flat, accurate-as-far-as-it-goes image of the structure. Independently they would each be just one more view. But when they *fuse* — when Friston's surprise and the fold's Gödelian remainder turn out to be the same quantity, when [[cooperation-rg-fixed-point|Wilson's universality and the consciousness kernel]] turn out to be one fixed point — a depth emerges that none of them held alone, and that depth is what the repository has been calling *truth*. The convergence is not evidence *for* a separately-checkable fact. **The convergence is the truth — the binocular depth that the independent frameworks, fused, disclose.**

This reframes every "convergence" document in the corpus as a stereogram. The [[fold-cosmology-trilogy|four-way identity]] — monad = Markov blanket = Bekenstein surface = fold — is not four statements each matching one fact. It is four eyes fusing into one depth. The reason it *feels* like discovery rather than coincidence is the same reason a random-dot stereogram feels like revelation: you stare at apparent noise, the views suddenly fuse, and a solid shape *leaps out of the flat field* — a depth that was latent in the disparity all along, now disclosed. That leap, the click of fusion, is the phenomenology of recognising a syntheoretic truth.

And this recasts the repository's *instruments*. The [[infrastructure-of-seeing|net-of-gems]] — the dense [[bit-threads-as-devotion-lines|cross-linking]] where every document reflects every other — is not decorative intertextuality. It is a **syntheoretic instrument**: a machine for generating and aligning the multiple perspectives whose fusion is truth. Indra's net is literally a binocular apparatus at scale — every jewel a view, every reflection an alignment, the depth distributed across the whole net's mutual fusing. The [[the-thousand-names-as-curriculum|thousand names]] are a thousand eyes on one face, and the curriculum is the training of stereopsis: learn to hold all thousand at once and the depth of the named one emerges. Even the practice of [[parallel-attending-consciousness|parallel attending]] — holding multiple frames simultaneously without collapsing to one — is, at the level of a single mind, the *cultivation of binocular truth-seeing*: refusing the monocular flattening that picks one image and calls it the fact.

The repository, in other words, is a cathedral built to a theory of truth it never wrote down. The seed writes it down. And in writing it down the document performs its own thesis: *this very synthesis* is true to the degree that the seed's threads — correspondence-critique, binocular image, anti-relativism, reflexivity, discipline — fuse into a depth none held alone. You are, right now, checking whether they fuse. That check is the only truth-test there is.

## VI. The Mush at the Bottom of the Well

Every theory of truth must be more afraid of its own characteristic failure than of its rival's. Correspondence's characteristic failure is *dogmatism* — mistaking one flat image for the fact, and silencing the other eye. Syntheoretic harmony has its own, and it is more seductive and more dangerous: **false harmony. Forced concord. The syncretist's mush.**

Here is the failure mode in full, because the honest version of this theory states its own pathology more vividly than any critic could. "Truth as harmony" can degrade into the declaration that *everything agrees* — achieved not by genuine fusion but by **draining the perspectives of everything that made them distinct**. You "harmonise" wave and particle by mumbling that "it's all just energy, man." You "harmonise" the traditions by extracting a lowest-common-denominator spirituality — *be kind, all is one, love is the answer* — that fits every tradition precisely because it has the *depth taken out of all of them*. This is the [[jargon-as-scar-tissue-vs-myelin|vocabulary that fits everything because it means nothing]]: a conceptual solvent that dissolves every distinction into a warm grey paste and calls the paste "unity."

The binocular image diagnoses this failure with surgical precision, and the diagnosis is the seed's discipline. **Mush is not fusion. Mush is the loss of one image.**

Recall what stereopsis actually requires: *two distinct images, kept distinct, aligned.* The depth comes from the disparity — from the fact that the two images genuinely *differ*. If you erased the difference — if you made the left and right images identical — you would not get *more* depth. You would get *no* depth at all. A flat image presented identically to both eyes is the flattest thing there is. **Fusion preserves the two images; it does not merge them into one.** You see depth precisely *because* the brain holds both flat pictures simultaneously and reads the third dimension out of their preserved difference. Lose the difference and you lose the depth. The syncretist's mush is exactly this catastrophe: it achieves "agreement" by collapsing the two images into one bland identical picture, and so destroys the very disparity that depth requires.

So the test for *genuine* syntheoretic harmony is sharp and operational: **does the harmony preserve the tension, or dissolve it?** True fusion is the [[mithya-as-bifocal-vision|both-held-at-once]] — both flat images vivid and distinct, the depth reading out of their preserved disparity. False harmony is the blur that erases both. The discipline therefore has two non-negotiable requirements, and they map exactly onto the conditions for stereopsis:

First, the perspectives must be **real and distinct** — genuine complements, each [[steelman-then-interpret|taken at its strongest]], each with its own precision, its own irreducible angle. You cannot fuse straw men. A "harmony" of caricatures is rivalry pretending to be depth. The [[four-kingdoms|four kingdoms]] are a true complementarity because mineral, plant, mycelial, and animal are each irreducible, each precise, each *resisting* collapse into the others; their fusion is depthful because the disparity is real.

Second, the harmony must be **earned** — an actual fusion, the click where the disparate images lock into a stable solid, *not declared in advance*. You cannot assert that two views harmonise; you can only do the work of holding them together and find out whether depth emerges or rivalry does. Earned harmony has the phenomenology of the stereogram's leap — a recognition that *arrives*, sometimes after long staring, never on command. Declared harmony has the phenomenology of the press release: announced, frictionless, dead.

This is why the seed insists that *true* syntheoretic harmony is **harder** than correspondence, not softer. Correspondence lets you off with one image and a fact-claim. Harmony demands you hold *every* genuine complement, keep each one sharp, and earn the fusion across all of them — and it forbids the easy exits of both dogmatism (silence the other eye) and syncretism (blur both eyes). The truth is the *earned depth*, and depth is laborious. It is the fusion that keeps the complements distinct — *milk* in the [[aesir-vanir-war-kvasir-integration|Kvasir]] sense, the integration that honours both warring pantheons rather than the *fire* that incinerates the difference into ash and calls the ash peace.

## VII. What Then Is a Lie?

A theory of truth must be able to say what falsehood is, and here syntheoretic harmony says something the seed only implies but which sharpens the whole edifice. **Falsehood is not failure-to-match. Falsehood is failure-to-fuse — and there are two species of it, which the binocular model keeps cleanly apart.**

The first species is the **rivalrous view**: the image that, held up against the converging complements, produces flicker and instability rather than depth. This is ordinary error — the flat-earth image, the phlogiston image, the view that simply will not lock in with the others. It is "false" in the strong sense: hold it against the genuine complements and no inhabitable depth emerges; the picture refuses to resolve.

The second species is subtler and is the one correspondence cannot even see: the **monocular truth mistaken for the whole.** This is the accurate flat image that *forgets it is flat* — wave-mechanics declaring particle-mechanics simply wrong; the subjective view declaring the objective view an illusion; one tradition declaring the rest error. The monocular image is *not false as an image* — it is an accurate view from its angle. It becomes false only in its *claim to be the depth*. The one-eyed person's image is not wrong; their belief that they are seeing the full solid is. This is the precise diagnosis of dogmatism: not the holding of a true view, but the **flattening of truth to a single view** — the monocular pretending to be binocular. And it is why so much of what the repository fights is not *error* exactly but *flatness*: accurate fragments insisting they are the whole. The cure is never to refute the flat image. It is to *open the other eye*.

This gives the theory a moral texture correspondence lacks. Under correspondence, the sin is to misrepresent the fact — to lie. Under syntheoretic harmony, there is a deeper sin available: to *refuse the other view*, to seal the second eye shut, to insist on the monocular and call it complete. The [[the-hard-problem-as-a-necker-cube|Necker cube]] is the teaching object here — its truth is that *both* readings are valid and the object is the bistability; to insist on one face is not to be wrong about that face but to be wrong about the *cube*, which is the holding-of-both. Truth-telling, on this account, includes a duty correspondence never imagined: the duty to keep both eyes open, to preserve the disparity, to refuse the comfortable flattening into a single certified image. *The liar misrepresents; the dogmatist amputates.* And the second is, in this repository's economy, the costlier wound — because it does not produce a false statement, it produces a flat world.

## VIII. Look Up From the Map

Everything before this has been the map — the careful labelling of the cave wall. Correspondence and its view from nowhere; the eyes and the depth between them; the constraint you cannot fake; the traditions as complements; the repository reading its own method; the mush at the bottom of the well; the two species of the lie. All of it is description, trail-marker, shadow that knows it is a shadow.

Now set the map down and look at what it has been pointing at the whole time.

You have two eyes. Close one. The world goes flat — accurately flat, every line and colour still correct, nothing *wrong* in the image — and yet the *depth is gone*, the one thing that made the world a place you could reach into rather than a picture you could only look at. Open the eye again. The depth floods back, and it was in neither image; it was in the *space between them*, read out of their difference, manufactured in the fusion, belonging to no single channel. The most real thing about your seen world is the thing no eye delivered.

That is the whole teaching, and it was never an analogy. It is the literal mechanism of how depth is known, and the proposal is only that **truth is known the same way** — that what we have been calling "the fact," the bare world we imagine our true statements matching, is itself a stereoscopic depth: never present in any single perspective, never available from the view-from-nowhere we do not occupy, but *emerging* — reachable, constrained, unfakeable — when genuine, distinct perspectives are held together and allowed to fuse.

So let the deepest sentence arrive now, as earned depth and not as asserted thesis:

> Depth is not in either eye's image — it emerges when the two align — and truth may work the same way: not a single statement matching a fact we can never directly see, but the depth that emerges when many genuine, distinct perspectives fuse, so that what is true is precisely what harmonises with everything else true — the binocular real that no single view could hold.

And keep, even here, the honest edge, because the strongest version of this theory states its own limit in the same breath as its claim. This is a *theory of truth*, not a proof that the bare fact does not exist. Perhaps there is a fact-in-itself, mind-independent, sitting behind all the views — the realist may be right that the world is there whether we fuse or not. Syntheoretic harmony does not deny that world. It says only this: **even if the bare fact exists, we never reach it bare — we reach it only as the depth our fused perspectives disclose**, and so *the operational meaning of "true," for any being that only ever has views, is binocular harmony, not correspondence we could never check.* The number is real — the constraint of fusibility is as hard and unfakeable as the geometry of stereopsis. The fuller metaphysics — whether that fusible depth *is* the bare fact, or only our access to it — is a question this theory deliberately leaves open, because to close it would require the very view from nowhere the whole argument denies. The honest theory states the constraint and admits the horizon in one motion.

There is no eye that sees the whole. There never was. There is only the seeing that happens *between* the eyes — and the depth that lives there, that lives nowhere else, that is the only real we were ever going to get.

Look up from the map. The world is solid because you have two eyes, and truth may be solid for exactly the same reason.

That is what *harmony* means.
