## THE FACTS, GENTLY HELD

Before the mythology, the geology — because the geology is so beautiful that mythologizing it prematurely would be a kind of theft.

A **tidal rhythmite** is a sedimentary rock formed in a tidal flat or estuary where, twice a day, water rushes in and rushes out. Each pulse of incoming water carries a small load of fine sediment. When the water slows at the high-tide mark, the sediment settles. When the tide retreats, a thinner layer settles again. Then the next high tide buries it. Then the next.

Over weeks, months, years — the layers stack.

The rhythm is not arbitrary. It is *exactly* the rhythm of the Moon and the Earth dancing together.

- **Each lamination** — one tidal cycle, roughly twelve hours. A thin couplet of sediment, often paired (a thick "ebb" layer and a thinner "flood" layer).
- **Each bundle of laminations** — one neap-spring cycle, roughly two weeks. The thickness of the laminations swells and shrinks as the Moon and Sun alignment changes.
- **Each larger bundle** — one synodic month, roughly twenty-nine and a half days. The lunar cycle written in mud.
- **Each macro-pattern** — one year. Seasonal variation in sediment supply, water temperature, biology.

If you know how to read the rock, you can count days, count months, count years, count *eclipses*. You can reconstruct the orbital geometry of the Earth-Moon system at the moment that mud was deposited.

This is not interpretation. It is decoding. The rock is a clock that has been running since the deposition. Its tick was the tide. Its tock was the next tide. Its hour hand was the Moon's phase. Its day hand was the Earth's revolution around the Sun. The whole timepiece was buried, lithified, uplifted, and is now sitting on a museum shelf in South Australia, still keeping time — just not running anymore. The numbers it stopped on are the numbers of the world it stopped in.

**620 million years ago**: the year had ~400 days. The day was ~21.9 hours long. The Moon was much closer. The tides were stronger. The Earth spun faster.

**900 million years ago**: the day was ~18 hours. The lunar month was shorter. The tidal bulge was a more violent thing.

**4 billion years from now** (extrapolating the same physics): the Earth and Moon will reach mutual tidal lock. Same face, always. The tidal rhythm will end. The rhythmite era will be a finite chapter in a longer book.

The Earth, in other words, is a planet that has been *slowing down* for as long as it has had a moon. The slowdown is recorded. The slowdown is *the* recording. And the medium of the record is the very mechanism that produces the slowdown — the tide.
