### Free energy principle as the formalisation of karma

Friston's free energy principle is older in spirit than it is in formal expression. The intuition that life is a process of holding entropy at bay by being a good model of one's environment goes back to Schrödinger's *What Is Life?*. Friston gave it teeth. Living systems exist by minimising free energy — the difference between sensory input and predicted input. They do this either by updating their model (perception) or by changing the world to match the model (action). Existence is sustained prediction.

The Buddhist account of karma is structurally identical, in a way that becomes obvious only once both languages are fluent. *Samskaras* — the conditioning impressions that arise from each volitional act — are the residue of unminimised surprise. Every action involves a model of how the world will respond. When the response matches expectation, the model is reinforced. When the response surprises, the system updates *or doubles down* — and the doubling-down is exactly what produces the karmic loop. The wheel keeps turning because surprise keeps generating residue, and residue keeps shaping the next prediction.

Liberation, in this register, is not escape from the wheel. It is the dissolution of the gap. When the model and the territory become identical, prediction error vanishes.
