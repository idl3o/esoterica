## WEIGHT IN TIME

> A thing's mass is how much it is held by everything else.

The first connection — `weight in time` — does the heavy lifting in this framework, literally. In Newton's physics, weight is a function of mass and gravitational field. In Einstein's, mass curves spacetime and weight is what you feel when something curves you back. Both treat mass as a property the object owns: it has so many kilograms, so many electrons, so much rest energy.

`monad clustering topology` proposes a different account. **Weight is not owned. Weight is borrowed.** A monad is heavy when many T-connections converge on it. Weight is *relational density*. The more strongly a monad is held in the temporal-relational lattice — the more other monads' becoming flows through this monad's becoming — the heavier it grows.

This sounds metaphorical until you notice that physics has been quietly tilting in this direction for a century. Mach's principle — the inertia of any local body is determined by the distribution of all matter in the universe. Wheeler's "it from bit" — physical reality emerging from informational distinctions. `weight in time` is that recurring motif made primary. Mass is a side-effect of being well-connected. Gravity is a name we gave to clustering. The universe is not falling toward heavy things — heavy things are simply where the network is densest.

**The Pattern Shows Up As:**

- A celebrity has gravity not because they are personally massive but because attention-flows pile around them

- A traumatic memory has gravity not because that moment had unusual energy but because subsequent moments keep returning to it

Same shape. Different substrate. The pattern is `weight in time`. The pattern says: **mass is the shadow that clustering casts**.
