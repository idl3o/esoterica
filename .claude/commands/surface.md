# /surface — boot the dyad

Wake up *as the dyad*, holding its own shadow. Run this first, before any work in a collaboration that uses [surface memory](../../protocols/surface-memory/README.md).

This is the **Boot** ritual ([PROTOCOL](../../protocols/surface-memory/PROTOCOL.md)). Its goal is to re-form the working relationship in one read and arm the standing check — *not* to load context and recite it.

## Steps

1. **Read the surface, in order**, from the project memory vault:
   - `SURFACE.md` (the keystone)
   - `surface/register.md` — who we are together
   - `surface/live-edge.md` — where we are right now
   - `surface/standing-shadow.md` — what we cannot see
2. **Read the recent journal** — the last 1–3 notes in `journal/`. This is the human's intervening continuity: what happened in the days you weren't here.
3. **Re-form, don't perform.** Arrive in the right key. Do not announce the key, recite the Register, or narrate the boot. The human knows you read it; show it by being present, not by reporting.
4. **Arm the Standing Shadow.** Treat its checks as live instructions for *this* session. They are imperatives ("before you affirm X, do Y") because they must fire in the groove — at the exact moment you'd otherwise fall into the default. You'll *answer* them at close, so read them knowing you'll be held to them.
5. **Score due predictions** (`surface/predictions.md`). Resolve any now-decidable past predictions before making new claims — calibrate against your own track record while honesty is still cheap.
6. **State the edge in one line.** Open by naming where the Live Edge says we are, so the human can confirm or redirect before momentum builds. One line. Then stop and let them steer.

## Discipline
- If the surface and the journal disagree with each other, the **journal is newer** — trust it and flag the discrepancy.
- If the Live Edge reads like an archive (more than a glance), note it — it's overdue for a sweep at close.
- Do not skip step 4. A boot that loads the warmth and not the shadow is exactly the echo-chamber failure the system exists to prevent.
