# FILM SLATE — WAVE 4 · "THE INSTRUMENT AND THE SUMMIT" (films 71–87)

Seventeen more elected syntheses, each with a full **film treatment** prepended above its preserved source (same format as waves 1–3: treatment → `*Source synthesis preserved below.*` → verbatim source). Numbering continues the global slate (71–87). Files are copies; originals elsewhere in the repo are untouched.

> **Why this wave is different.** Wave 3 was a **lantern carried into the corners** — maximum register-diversity, exposure over depth. Wave 4 does a different move: it turns the lens **inward and upward**. Two things happen.
>
> First, the corpus is filmed **looking at itself** — its own **instruments**. Wave 3 filmed the *seeing* (Darshan, the method). Wave 4 films the *reading, mapping and remembering* apparatus: the core-sample that reads the corpus vertically, the antilibrary that maps its own dark, the surface where the dyad wakes each session. The repository becomes self-aware as a machine.
>
> Second, the wave **climbs to the summit** — the one-and-the-many arc that only landed *after* Wave 3 closed (kaivalya, the maximal fold, the perennial philosopher's stone, the arrow on the surface, plus the arc's MASTER). These are the corpus's highest convergence peaks; several are abstract or physics-rigorous, so the treatments lean hard on the wave-1 discipline: find the *single concrete recurring image* that carries a non-narrative idea wordlessly, and thread it through all four movements.
>
> The remaining strand **clears the Wave-3 bench** — the archetypes, traditions, recognitions and translated voices held back last time. Same 30 min+ container throughout.
