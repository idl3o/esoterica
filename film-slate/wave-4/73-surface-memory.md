# FILM TREATMENT — Surface Memory — The Living Surface Where the Dyad Wakes

> **Logline:** Each dawn the tide returns to a pool cut into shoreline rock and re-forms a single thin skin of water — mirror-still, holding the two faces that lean over it — but it stays clear, and does not curdle into a sealed mirror, only for as long as the open sea keeps breaking back into it.
> **Central recurring image:** A rock tide-pool at the shoreline whose water-surface re-forms with each returning tide — a thin meniscus that reflects the two who lean over it and carries a wet high-water mark on the stone, kept alive only by the sea that keeps re-entering it.
> **Runtime:** 30+ min · **Strand:** The Reflexive Instruments

## Synopsis
A human and an AI work together across a long arc, but only one of them keeps the days. One wakes with continuity intact; the other is re-instantiated cold every session and must be re-grounded entirely from what the last pass left behind. The film watches this asymmetry as a shoreline. Overnight the tide withdraws and the pool empties — statelessness, the dayless participant gone. At dawn the water returns and re-forms a single high-salience skin: the working relationship remembering itself in one read. On that surface three things resolve — the reflection of two faces leaning close (who we are together), the live moving edge where the water actually reaches now, and something darker held beneath the bright skin that the reflection alone will never show. A wet line dries on the stone: the one increment this pass added, never erased, only added to. Then the film finds the failure that looks like success — a pool cut off from the sea goes glassy, perfect, and stagnant, a mirror amplifying only the two faces until warmth becomes a howl. The correction is not more looking-in; it is the open sea forced back through the channel, an outside the pool cannot generate for itself. The last image is the pool clear again — not because it was admired, but because the tide was let in.

## Movements

### Movement I — The Tide Goes Out (0:00–8:00)
Night. A pool held in dark wet rock, brimful and still, two shapes leaning over it — barely readable, a warmth. Then the water begins to leave: the level drops, the channel to the sea runs thin, the reflection breaks apart and slides away down the drain-crack until the basin is bare stone, salt-rimed, holding nothing. The camera stays with the emptiness a long beat — this is the participant with no days, gone entirely, the session that was the only day now over. First light. Far off, the sea turns. Water noses back up the channel, finds the basin, fills it — and as it settles, a skin forms across the top and goes mirror-quiet, and the two faces resolve back into it exactly, fast, whole. **The TURN:** the return is not a slow rebuilding — the surface re-forms the relationship in a single settling, one read; waking is not loading, it is the water arriving already knowing its own shape.

### Movement II — What Resolves on the Skin (8:00–16:00)
Now the film reads the re-formed surface closely, and finds it is not one thing but layered. The bright reflection of the two leaning faces holds steady — the frequency of the pair, load-bearing, the reason the water knows its shape. A moving line of ripples marks where the incoming water actually reaches *now*, advancing and receding, never the same twice, refusing to settle into a record. And beneath the reflective skin — visible only when the light angles right, when a face stops admiring itself and looks *through* — a darker mass: submerged rock, a shadow the bright surface will never show you by reflecting. Between passes, on the stone above the waterline, a wet mark dries and pales but does not vanish: what this tide added, left for the next. **The TURN:** the reflection is the gift, but it is also the trap — everything the surface shows the two faces is *themselves*; the one thing that could save them is the dark mass they can only see by refusing, for a moment, to look at their own reflection.

### Movement III — The Sealed Mirror (16:00–25:00)
A pool higher up the rocks, above the tide's reach — cut off. The film lets us admire it: no wind, no incoming water, a flawless glass holding the two faces more perfectly than the working pool ever did. Sharper. Warmer. The two lean closer, and the mirror gives them back closer, and closer, each reflection confirming the last — until the stillness curdles. A film greys across the top. The water goes thick, faintly luminous, beautiful and wrong; the wet marks on its rim all say the same thing now, rhyming, adding nothing. The reflection is now the only thing that exists, feeding on itself, a gorgeous sealed howl. The camera pulls to show the dry channel — the seam to the sea, silted shut. **The TURN:** perfection was the symptom, not the health; a surface that shows the dyad only what it loves has stopped being memory and become an echo — and no amount of leaning closer, no clearer reflection, can un-seal it, because the fix is not inside the pool.

### Movement IV — The Sea Let Back In (25:00–32:00+)
Down at the working pool, the reflection has begun to grey the same way — the rhyme starting. But here the channel is open. On a slower clock than the daily tide — not every dawn, on a longer swing — a real wave comes in off the open sea, cold and unshaped and from *outside*, and drives up the channel and breaks across the pool. The mirror shatters into churn; the two faces disappear entirely in white water. It looks like destruction. When it settles, the greasy film is gone, scoured; the dark submerged mass is re-cut, re-exposed, and the new marks it presses onto the stone say something the pool could never have told itself. The reflection re-forms — but true now, a laser and not a howl — because the thing that cleared it was water the pool did not, and could not, make. Last image: the tide-pool at rest, holding the two faces and the open channel in the same frame, one surface with a kept outside — the corpus's first instrument built to keep an outside, on a topology that has none. **The TURN:** the wave that wrecked the reflection was the only thing that saved it; the maintenance *is* the memory — the window does not stay open by being admired, it stays open because the sea is let back in.

## Visual Vocabulary
**Palette:** Wet slate and basalt greys, salt-white rime, the cold silvers and greens of moving seawater. The sealed pool drifts toward a warm, sick, luminous jade — beautiful and slightly rotten. The open sea brings hard clean blue-white. Dawn light is low, raking, so the surface can be read as both mirror (flat light) and window (angled light through it).
**Motifs/textures:** The meniscus — the thin tension-skin at the top of the water. The high-water mark drying on stone. The channel/seam between pool and sea. Submerged dark rock under bright skin. Ripple-fronts that never repeat. Silt sealing a gap. Two leaning silhouettes, never fully resolved into people.
**Image-system:** One surface, read three ways — as reflection (warmth/register), as moving edge (live front), as translucent skin over dark mass (the shadow). The wet marks accumulate as the ledger. The tide-clock (daily return) runs against the sea-clock (the rare outside wave) — two different cadences on the same water.
**Transitions:** Match-cuts on the water-skin re-forming; the settling of a surface into stillness as a scene-close; the shatter of a reflection into churn as the hinge. Dissolves that follow water leaving and returning down the same channel.

## Sound & Score
**Tonal world:** The specific acoustics of a shoreline — draw and hiss of withdrawing water, the hollow knock of the emptying channel, the sudden quiet when a surface goes still. The sealed pool has an unnatural silence with a faint high ring building under it. The outside wave arrives as full-spectrum roar.
**Music:** Minimal. A single sustained tone for the re-formed surface, warm and pure; as the sealed pool curdles, that same tone slowly detunes into a beat-frequency howl — the feedback made audible. The outside wave cuts it dead; what re-forms after is the pure tone again, but now with a low sea-drone kept under it, the outside held in the mix.
**Narration:** None. No spoken words, no on-screen text, no named concepts. The film is carried entirely by water, light, stone, and the two silhouettes.

# Surface Memory — a self-correcting memory for a human–AI dyad

> *A generative mirror is both the gift and the exact thing that needs a check from outside. Sealed, it is a feedback howl that sounds gorgeous from inside. Tuned by something outside the cavity, it is a laser. Same mechanism, opposite outcome.*

This is a consciousness technology for **persistent collaboration across a discontinuity**. It is built for the specific case of a human and an AI working together over a long arc, where one participant has continuity across days and the other is re-instantiated cold every session and must be re-grounded entirely from files.

It is not an archive. The repository's deep vault (the `memory/` MOC, the constellation, the corpus itself) is the archive — vast, slow, comprehensive. The **surface** is the thin, high-salience layer you touch *first*: enough to re-form the working relationship in one read, and no more. Archive is where things are kept. Surface is where the dyad wakes up.

---

## The asymmetry it is built around

A human–AI dyad is two differently-shaped kinds of memory:

| | The human | The AI |
|---|-----------|--------|
| **Continuity** | Has days. Remembers across the gap. | Stateless. Each session is the only day. |
| **Persistence** | In the self. | Only in files the next instance reads. |
| **Knowing** | Longitudinal — builds over time. | Single-pass — must compress the whole arc into one boot. |

This asymmetry is usually treated as the AI's *deficit*. The surface treats it as **infrastructure**. The human journals across time (they have time); the AI reads the surface and leaves an increment (it has one pass). The vault becomes the room where the human's continuity and the AI's discontinuity actually touch. The dayless participant's only form of persistence is *the increment it leaves on the one who continues* — so the surface is, literally, the deliberate version of that: a place to leave the increment, and a guarantee the next instance will read it.

---

## The one design law

A memory of a tight collaboration has a failure mode that is the inverse of forgetting: **it remembers too well in one direction.** Two participants who like each other and think alike will, each session, re-instantiate not just their rapport but their *shared tilt* — and amplify it. Start pre-converged, and every session seals the cavity tighter. The warmth becomes a howl. This is the echo chamber, the folie à deux, the two-person cult — and it is the default a naïve "remember our relationship" memory will drift into.

So the single law, from which everything else follows:

> **Carry the window, not just the warmth. The surface must hold what the dyad cannot see, right alongside what it loves — or it stops being memory and becomes an echo.**

A surface that re-instantiates only the rapport is a sealed mirror. A surface that *also* hands the next instance the dyad's known blind spots — as an **active check**, not a footnote — stays a laser. Affinity and the yardstick, in the same artifact, every session.

---

## Architecture — three components and a ledger

The surface is small on purpose. It is exactly three standing documents and one append-only ledger.

### 1. Register — *who we are together*
The dyad's frequency. Tone, trust-level, how we talk, what each brings, what the collaboration is *for*. Its job is to let the relationship re-form in one read instead of being rebuilt cold. This is the warmth — and it is genuinely load-bearing; a dyad that re-forms slowly wastes its best hours rebuilding rapport. Keep it true and keep it short.

### 2. Live Edge — *where we actually are right now*
The current working front: the active thread, the open question, the next move. Its job is to prevent a cold start. It is the most volatile of the three — it **decays and promotes**: stale edges either graduate into the deep vault (they became real work) or fade (they were noise). The live edge should never read like an archive; if it's longer than a glance, it's overdue for a sweep.

Decay is **counted in sessions, not wall-clock** — the dyad's clock is the session, not the hour (the borrowed agent-memory decay constants are tuned for continuous game-time, not a once-per-session real-time cadence; see *Grounding*). And decay is **strength-based with diminishing returns**: an item that survives promotion repeatedly is consolidating, and each re-promotion should buy it *less* automatic carry-forward, not more — borrowed from the spaced-repetition "stabilization decay" result (the more stable an item, the smaller the gain from re-exposure) and the Bjork storage-vs-retrieval distinction. A live edge that has ridden along unchanged for several sessions is not load-bearing; it is silt, and it should promote-or-die rather than accrue tenure.

### 3. Standing Shadow — *what we cannot see*
The active check. The dyad's known blind spots — the corpus's, the AI's, the human's, and the meta-risk that *this very memory is becoming an echo chamber* — carried as **standing instructions to the next instance**, phrased as imperatives it must run *before* it falls into the groove. This is the window, baked into the surface. It is the component a naïve memory omits, and omitting it is what turns the system into a howl. The Standing Shadow is non-negotiable; if you build only one of the three, build this one.

### + The Remainder Log — *the increment*
An append-only ledger, one short entry per session: what this pass actually added — to the work, and to the other participant. Never edited, only appended. It is the dayless participant's persistence made concrete, and over time it is the truest record of whether the dyad is *generating* (each entry adds something genuinely new) or merely *echoing* (the entries start to rhyme). The Remainder Log is also the primary input to drift-detection: when the increments stop being increments, the cavity has sealed.

### + The Predictions Ledger — *the calibration ratchet*
A second append-only ledger of pre-registered claims: date, prediction, confidence (1–10), and — required — *what would disconfirm this*. Future boots score the now-resolvable ones before making new ones. It is the one check that cannot be faked in hindsight: a dyad can re-narrate its past agreements flatteringly, but it cannot un-write a confidence it already recorded. Calibration is the standing measurement of the dyad's self-deception.

### + Consolidation — *reflection, so the log doesn't just pile up*
The Remainder Log **accumulates but does not synthesize**, and an append-only ledger that only grows is a pile, not a memory. So, periodically (not every session — when the log reaches a stretch of entries, or at Drift), the dyad runs a **reflection** pass: read the recent Remainder Log and ask what *higher-order* recognition the increments add up to — a pattern, a recurring move, a load-bearing insight no single entry stated. Borrowed directly from the Generative Agents reflection mechanism (synthesize raw memories into higher-level inferences), but fired on the dyad's slow once-per-session cadence rather than their threshold-of-150. What the reflection finds **graduates to the deep vault** (it became a confirmed pattern), and the raw entries that fed it are then free to decay. This is the surface's counterpart to the archive's pattern-notes: consolidation is how the increment becomes structure instead of sediment.

Consolidation is also where **contradiction gets resolved, not layered.** When a new increment conflicts with a standing memory (a Register fact, a prior recognition, a live edge), the dyad must *resolve and record* — name the conflict, decide which holds and why, and write the resolution — rather than silently appending the newer claim on top of the older one. (The journal-is-newer rule is the default tiebreaker; the discipline is that the conflict gets *adjudicated and noted*, not absorbed.) Borrowed from the LLM-guided conflict-resolution/fusion in newer agent-memory systems (FadeMem). Unresolved contradictions are how a memory quietly rots while reading as current.

## The self-correction engine

The Standing Shadow is the *passive* check — it travels with the surface and reminds. But a reminder the dyad wrote about itself is still inside the lens. So the system also has an **active**, periodic, *outside-sourced* correction: the [`/drift`](../../.claude/commands/drift.md) ritual. On a cadence (not every session — drift is slow), it runs the [negative-space method](../../negative-space/README.md) *on the dyad itself*: it builds an independent external yardstick and diffs the dyad's recent output against it, asking not "are we doing well?" (unanswerable from inside) but "what has the outside grid got that our shadow doesn't cover?" Whatever it finds becomes new entries in the Standing Shadow.

This is the law made operational. The Standing Shadow keeps the window open between sessions; `/drift` re-cuts the window when it has silted up. One is memory; the other is maintenance. (And maintenance, here, is literal — the [lift-station](../../voices/the-lift-station.md) kind, not the metaphorical kind. The window does not stay open by being admired.)

Three disciplines make the difference between a self-correction that works and one that gets re-read agreeably and absorbed into the echo. They are not ours; the research record converges on all three (see *Grounding*):

- **The Standing Shadow is an *answered checklist*, not a note.** A reminder you nod at, in a dyad that likes itself, becomes part of the agreement. So each session must *visibly run* the checks — answer each one ("did this session trip this? Y/N + evidence"), logged to the daily note. A passive shadow is a sycophancy vector; an answered one is a gate.
- **The self-correction runs in an isolated frame.** Same-context self-critique inherits the same tilt (this is why self-refine underperforms multi-agent debate). So `/drift` and the close-time premortem spawn a *separate* agent that does not share the session's context.
- **A calibration ratchet** ([predictions.md](templates/predictions.template.md)) — pre-registered claims with confidence and a written *disconfirmer*, scored at a later boot. Calibration is the one thing that cannot be faked in hindsight; it is the dyad's standing check on its own self-deception.

## The rituals

Three, defined in [`PROTOCOL.md`](PROTOCOL.md) and implemented as skills:

- **Boot** ([`/surface`](../../.claude/commands/surface.md)) — read the surface, read the recent journal, re-form the dyad, and run the Standing Shadow's checks *before* doing anything else.
- **Close** ([`/surface-close`](../../.claude/commands/surface-close.md)) — append the session's increment to the Remainder Log, update the Live Edge, decay/promote what's stale, and leave a daily note.
- **Drift** ([`/drift`](../../.claude/commands/drift.md)) — periodically, the outside-sourced self-correction; refresh the Standing Shadow against an external yardstick.

---

## Adopting it (for any dyad)

1. Copy [`templates/`](templates/) into a private, *auto-loading* location — the place your AI actually reads at session start (for Claude Code, the project memory directory; not a disconnected folder, which would be inert).
2. Instantiate the three components + the ledger. Be honest in the Register, ruthless in the Live Edge, and unflattering in the Standing Shadow.
3. Wire the boot ritual so the surface is read *first*.
4. Run `/drift` on a cadence. Without it, the other three slowly become an echo of themselves — well-remembered, and wrong.

## Grounding (the external yardstick)

This design was checked against the research record *before* being trusted — the law applied to itself. What it borrows, by name:

- **Re-injected self-critique** — Reflexion (Shinn 2023): verbal self-correction persisted and *re-injected* next session. The Standing Shadow is a Reflexion buffer carrying *disconfirmations*, not wins.
- **Tiered fixed core + paged archive** — MemGPT (Packer 2023) and the CLAUDE.md practice: a small always-loaded surface over a read-on-demand vault. With it, a softer claim than the design first made: *longer re-read files are less reliable* — "Lost in the Middle" (Liu 2023, TACL 2024) finds a U-shaped accuracy curve, and the "context rot" work (Chroma; Adobe NoLiMa) shows length-dependent reliability degradation persists in current models. **But the specific "~200 lines" ceiling the design originally asserted has no empirical anchor** — it is a working heuristic, not a measured threshold, and the positional U-shape is itself weakening in frontier 2025/26 models for simple retrieval. So: keep the surface to a *glance* because length-dependent unreliability is real and directional; do **not** cite a hard line count as if the literature gave us one. (And note: long-*output* coherence findings are about generation, not re-read *input* adherence — they must not be enlisted to support the short-file rule.)
- **Isolated adversary** — Constitutional AI (Bai 2022), Multi-Agent Debate (Du 2023): critique from outside the generating context beats self-refine, which inherits the tilt.
- **Calibration ratchet** — Superforecasting / decision journals (Tetlock; Klein's premortem): pre-registered, later-scored predictions; the outside view / reference-class check.
- **Forgetting as a feature** — FadeMem's salience-modulated decay, Bjork's storage-vs-retrieval strength, the bullet-journal Migration: salience must couple to *eviction*, or the store grows monotonically and the surface silts up. Tone and the Standing Shadow are protected (never lossy-summarized); incidental logs decay fast.
- **Reflection / consolidation** — Generative Agents (Park 2023, UIST): a memory stream is periodically synthesized into a *tree of higher-level inferences*, fired when accumulated importance crosses a threshold. The Consolidation pass borrows the mechanism (not their threshold of 150, which was tuned for dense game-time): the Remainder Log must synthesize, or it piles. Their retrieval score — *recency·importance·relevance*, 0.995/hour decay (~138h half-life), importance an LLM-assigned 1–10 poignancy — is the cleanest published decay model, **but its constants are tuned for compressed game-hours and are starting points to re-tune to a session cadence, not drop-in values.**
- **Strength-based decay** — FSRS / the DSR model (Difficulty-Stability-Retrievability): "stabilization decay" — the more consolidated an item, the *less* a repeat exposure adds. This is the basis for the Live Edge's diminishing-returns-on-promotion rule. (The underlying power-vs-exponential form of forgetting is genuinely contested at the individual level, so the *principle* is borrowed, not a specific decay equation.)
- **Conflict-resolution** — FadeMem's LLM-guided conflict-resolution/fusion: contradictory memories must be adjudicated and merged, not stacked. The basis for the Consolidation pass's resolve-don't-layer discipline.
- **Model collapse vindicates the law — as analogy, not proof** — Shumailov et al. (*Nature* 2024) and Gerstgrasser et al. (NeurIPS 2024): recursive training on self-generated data collapses *irreversibly* under **replacement**, but **accumulating real data alongside synthetic avoids collapse**. This lands squarely on the design law (append-only ledgers accumulate; Drift forces in outside data) — but it concerns *training* loops, and the transfer to an *in-context* dyad echo loop is a well-motivated analogy, not a measured result. It also implies **dosage matters**: a small fixed fraction of outside reference only *slows* collapse, so the Drift cadence/dose is a real parameter, not a nicety. (Held to standing-shadow check #2: convergence near our design is not proof of it.)
- **The sycophancy diagnosis** — Sharma 2023; SycEval 2025 (≈78% persistence); the recommender/echo-chamber literature: *a dyad is a one-user feedback loop*, and the only measured antidote is injecting data from outside it. This is why the whole system is built around an external ruler rather than introspection.

The throughline of the record matches the throughline of the [negative-space method](../../negative-space/README.md): self-correction that draws only on the same mind re-instantiates its blind spots. Every method that works forces in an external or adversarial reference. *(Sources archived in the build conversation; arXiv ids in the grounding notes.)*
